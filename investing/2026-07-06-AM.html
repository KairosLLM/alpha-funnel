Micron gapped up 7% before the bell and Michael Burry is short it, and the space between the invoice and the skeptic is the entire tape this morning

The memory melt-up ran straight into a famous bear on the first session back from the holiday, the crypto side is bleeding out in silence, and three fresh names (Microsoft, AeroVironment, Veeva) showed up cheap. Here is what to watch into the open.

StocksMacroBitcoinCrypto

Two moods are fighting for the tape and they could not be further apart. Semis and memory are gapping up like the supercycle is a settled fact, while crypto is quietly down 50 to 87% from its highs and nobody is talking about it. The morning's job is to hold both in your head without letting either one size your book for you. Here is the open, then the six things that matter.

The open. Futures are green and the memory complex is doing the heavy lifting. Per the Market list, $MU is up about 7% overnight after breaking ground on a $9.3B HBM plant in Hiroshima (high-bandwidth memory, the stacked chips that feed AI accelerators) with $3.1B of Japanese government support, and $SOXX, $SMH, and $SNDK are all set to open firm. Keep yesterday's $SPY map: 755 is the breakout, 740 is the breakdown, per Stocks with Josh.

Three dates own the week. The FOMC minutes land Wednesday July 8 and get read for whether a hike is really coming, the SK Hynix US listing debuts Friday July 10 under $SKHY as a second pure memory play, and $AVAV holds an Investor Day Wednesday July 8. Gold is stalling just above $4,200 as traders scale back Fed hike bets, per the Financial Markets list, and oil is lower on an OPEC+ output increase.

1. The memory melt-up got an invoice and a short-seller in the same session. The bull case keeps getting more concrete. Per the Stocks list, Gavin Baker is now framing memory as the cheapest thing in the AI stack at 3 to 5x earnings, with only three HBM makers on earth and Micron's contracts covering about half its revenue at floor prices above the last cycle's peak margins. And per Lark Davis, Micron's own CEO Sanjay Mehrotra is stretching the runway further, arguing a humanoid robot carries ten times the memory of a self-driving car and that the real multi-decade demand cycle has not even started.

Then the other shoe: Michael Burry has disclosed a short on $MU, and separately, per the Polymarket list, he is calling the whole AI semiconductor complex a bubble about to pop. One live risk to file under the bull case, per the Financial Markets list: China's CXMT is testing next-generation bonded DRAM and closing the gap with Korea faster than expected. That is the one thing that could crack the three-supplier scarcity story.

2. Nvidia's Kyber slipped a year, and that repriced the whole optics supply chain. The under-owned story this morning is a rack delay. Per Melvin, Nvidia's Kyber NVL144 (the 144-chip successor to the current Blackwell rack that Jensen demoed at GTC three months ago) is now reportedly pushed more than 12 months to 2028 on co-packaged optics yield problems, and the NVL72x2 bridge rack was cancelled outright. The read-through: $AMD, $MRVL, and $AVGO all benefit as hyperscalers accelerate their own custom silicon when Nvidia's roadmap slips. The sharper single-name call is $AAOI, which the market treats as a co-packaged optics play but which actually makes most of its money on pluggable transceivers, and a Kyber delay keeps those pluggables bid for two more years.

3. The neoclouds that got crushed on Friday caught a bid overnight. The complex that had its worst two days since COVID is trying to bounce. Per the Stocks list, $NBIS is up about 4% overnight, and $IREN is being called the 2026 bottom on the back of a reported 5-gigawatt, 500,000-GPU Nvidia partnership that puts a $500 target in the conversation, with $CRWV trading near 2.85x forward sales. This is the same high-beta, balance-sheet-sensitive end of the AI trade that broke first on the Meta headline, and a two-day bounce does not fix the leverage. Own it on the flush, never with size into the rip.

4. Microsoft is the cheapest it has been in five years, and the drawdown is almost entirely multiple. A fresh name worth real attention. Per one detailed Reddit breakdown, $MSFT sits near $373 to $390 at a 19.3x forward multiple, roughly 40% below its three-year average, with the entire 33% drawdown coming from multiple compression rather than any business crack. The fundamentals are still elite: Azure grew 40%, the AI business is at a $37B annualized run rate up 123% year over year, operating margin is 46%, and return on equity is 34%.

Per a second write-up, a base-case DCF lands fair value around $580 to $600, though the honest risk is the roughly $190B of 2026 capex crushing free cash flow, plus a restructured OpenAI deal that ended Azure's model exclusivity. Earnings hit July 29.

5. Bitcoin's bottom call split cleanly in two overnight. You have two credible reads pointing opposite ways, and that split is the whole decision. Per Benjamin Cowen, the four-year cycle is replaying 2018 at exactly 10x the price, the local low of $57,000 already printed on July 1, and a July window of strength should carry Bitcoin back toward its 200-day average before it rolls over into a Q4 low. Against that, per Stocks with Josh, the bottom is not in, $69,000 is the level that invalidates the downtrend, and until price closes above it he wants to short the rejection there.

Under both calls the damage is already brutal. Per Wajahat Mughal, Bitcoin is down 50% from its high, Ether 64%, and the alts are a graveyard with $SOL down 72% and $AAVE down 87%. The long-term case has not changed, and per Jeff Park Bitcoin has still existed for only about 7% of the time the United States has, but that is cold comfort to anyone marked to market this morning.

6. Saylor sold Bitcoin for the first time in four years, and the preferred is where the stress shows. The MicroStrategy story turned genuinely two-sided. Per Lark Davis, $MSTR has fallen to an $82 two-year low even though it still holds 847,000 Bitcoin worth roughly $55 to $60B, and Saylor sold a token 32 coins, likely for compliance reasons, while authorizing a larger monetization program. The real pressure is in the $STRC preferred, which trades at $71 against a promised $100 as its dividend obligations quadrupled from $300M to $1.2B in six months and coverage runway collapsed from seven years to fourteen months.

The bull answer is Saylor's own math that those dividends survive 40 years even at zero Bitcoin appreciation, but Peter Schiff is calling it finished and JP Morgan now labels him a risk to Bitcoin itself.

Per this write-up, the life-sciences software vendor owns more than 80% of pharma CRM seats, carries zero debt and over $5B net cash, and has compounded free cash flow per share above 35% a year while the market has left it unloved. No catalyst named, a watch-and-wait, but a quality name going cheap.
  • $PFE (Pfizer). Per Stocks with Josh, a fresh swing idea with a stated entry near $23.50, trading below its pre-COVID price at a 6 to 7% dividend with the revenue-cliff fear largely priced in. A slow accumulate, not a lump-sum chase.
  • $SOFI. Per the People-to-follow list, the loan-platform take rate is running near 4.6% on about $12B annualized originations at roughly 15x 2028 earnings with 30%-plus member growth. Reports July 29.

Psychology. Look at what the tape is asking you to feel this morning. Memory and semis are gapping up, the timeline is full of people posting $1,800 and $3,000 targets on $MU and $SNDK, and the pull is to chase the green candle before the bell so you do not miss it. That is exactly the moment Michael Burry chose to disclose a short and call the whole AI-semi complex a bubble, and per the Crypto list even Pentosh1 admitted he did not buy the memory dip on Friday. When the crowd is euphoric and one of the few people who called the last blowup is fading it, the risk is not being wrong on the thesis. The risk is oversizing into strength because the strength feels safe.

Now look at the other screen. Crypto is down 50 to 87% across the board, Saylor is selling for the first time in four years, and per the Crypto list Novogratz is saying nobody cares about crypto anymore. That silence is the opposite emotion, the flat apathy that shows up near lows and makes you want to close the tab and stop looking.

The discipline today is to run both feelings through the same rule. Per Steve Burns, risk a fixed unit per trade and count your outcomes in R, not dollars, so a hot memory name and a bombed-out alt are the same size of bet and neither the euphoria nor the despair gets to move your hand. The people who blow up in a tape like this are not the ones who pick wrong. They are the ones who felt certain and let the feeling set the size.
