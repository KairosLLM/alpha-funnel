SK Hynix debuts on the Nasdaq today as the biggest US listing ever by a foreign company, and overnight the Iran oil panic that ran the tape yesterday quietly reversed

The memory IPO priced at $149 for a $26.5 billion raise that topped Alibaba, and it trades when-issued this morning under SKHYV. While it did, oil slid on fresh US-Iran deal talk, the yen roared on a repatriation pledge, and every member of the Magnificent Seven except Apple sits in a double-digit drawdown. Here is the pre-open.

Yesterday afternoon the story was Trump killing the ceasefire and oil ripping toward $80. Overnight it flipped. Trump said Iran called to make a deal, crude slid, the yen soared, and the whole risk picture cooled into the one event the market actually cares about this morning, the largest US listing ever by a foreign company. Here is the pre-open, then the six things that matter into a Friday with Delta reporting and CPI next week.

The pre-open. Set up green but thin. The cash close yesterday was strong, with the S&P 500 up 0.81 percent to 7,543.64 and the Nasdaq up 1.30 percent to 26,206.89 per the People list, and semis led with the SMH up 2.5 percent, Micron up 4.5 percent, and Sandisk up 7.6 percent. Overnight the macro backdrop softened in the bulls' favor. Per the Financial Markets list, gold slipped below its perch above $4,100 on less hawkish Fed minutes and fresh US-Iran optimism, and in Tokyo the 10-year JGB yield fell 10 basis points to 2.775 percent with the yen soaring, adding about $174 billion to Japanese stocks at the open. Per Stocks with Josh, jobless claims came in strong at 215,000, Delta reports this morning as the first real read on the consumer, and next week's CPI is the number that decides the tape into big-tech earnings. The SPY has to close above 752 today to keep its breakout alive.

1. SK Hynix debuts today, and the book is the biggest ever for a foreign name. This is the event. Per the People list, the SK Hynix ADR priced at $149, sold 177.9 million shares to raise $26.5 billion, and drew demand near $200 billion, making it the largest first-time US share sale by a foreign company ever, past Alibaba's $25 billion in 2014. The top 10 accounts took about half the deal. It trades when-issued this morning under SKHYV and converts to regular-way trading under SKHY on Monday, with the leveraged single-stock ETFs starting Tuesday and options later in the week. Trading is expected to open around midday, so the opening print does not exist yet, and anyone quoting one before then is guessing.

There are two cleaner ways to play it than chasing the open. Per the Stocks list, SK Square holds a treasury that is mostly SK Hynix stock and trades at roughly a 49 percent discount to that stake, with the US listing as the re-rate catalyst, and the Korea proxy is the KOSPI itself, now bouncing off its weekly trend with 6,800 to 7,100 as the line that is either defended here or lost for a long time.

2. The overnight reversal cooled oil and split the Fed read. Respect the flip. Per the People list, crude slid after Trump said Iran had called to make a deal with Qatar and Pakistan mediating, the exact opposite of yesterday afternoon's oil-toward-$80 move. Per the Financial Markets list, the softer tone came with the yen repatriation trade, as Katayama pledged to push the GPIF into Japanese assets and JGB yields fell across the curve. The catch is the Fed is not sending one signal. Ed Yardeni read the same minutes as confirming a hawkish pivot with the S&P stuck near 7,500, and Verdence's Megan Horneman still warns the market is underpricing a second-half rate hike. A cooler Middle East helps stocks, but it does not settle whether the next Fed move is a hike.

3. Sandisk is the new memory leader, and the whole complex is leaning one way. The standout was not Micron. Per the People list, Sandisk ran about 9 percent intraday to close up 7.6 percent on a leaked Meta memo detailing multi-year flash-storage supply plans, and Wedbush lifted its target to $2,000 against a stock already up roughly 800 percent this year. Micron closed up 4.5 percent and Barchart's line is that it will make more money in 2026 than in the prior 35 years combined. Per the Stocks list, the fresh Samsung angle is a reported talk with Anthropic to make a custom AI chip on Samsung's 2nm node. The entire memory and semi trade is now crowded to the same side into the Hynix print, and that is exactly when a leader like Sandisk at 800 percent becomes the risk as much as the reward.

4. Meta set up for July 29, and the Mag 7 is quietly in a drawdown. The megacap tape has a clean level and a warning underneath it. Per the Stocks list, Meta hit $639.36 after hours and finished up 4 percent, with resistance stacked at 642, 656, 673, and 691, a close stop at $615, and targets of $700 to $750 into its July 29 earnings, where the sell side now models more than $200 billion of 2027 capex. Nvidia is pointed at Heisenberg's 208.65 to 210 near-term target, matching yesterday's upside line. The caution is breadth. Per the Financial Markets list, Ben Carlson notes every Magnificent Seven name except Apple is in a double-digit drawdown from its high, with Microsoft down 29 percent, Meta down 20, Tesla down 17, and Nvidia down 14. The index is holding while its generals are bleeding, and that gap closes one way or the other.

5. The power trade got called a gift, and a bear put a number on the glut. The neocloud fight sharpened. Per the People list, Morgan Stanley's Stephen Byrd called last week's power-stock selloff completely wrong and a gift, with TeraWulf trading below $4 per watt against $19 of deal value and Cipher Mining carrying a $48.50 target versus $23, on new deals at 16 to 20 percent yields. TeraWulf is seeking $3.5 billion to fund a data center leased to Anthropic and is up 81 percent over six months. Per the Stocks list, IREN starts delivering its Horizon 1 site to Microsoft in about 10 days, turning on revenue from its contracted book. The bear got specific too. Per the Financial Markets list, Bernstein now counts 12 years to clear the 338 gigawatt data-center pipeline, up from 10 a month ago, and that is the glut case in one number.

6. Bitcoin holds while Robinhood's chain eats Hyperliquid. The structure firmed and the venue map shifted. Per the Crypto list, Bitcoin is holding around $61,300 to $63,000, and Matt Hougan reframed the Saylor scare, that Strategy sold $200 million and the price barely moved because Strategy is no longer the marginal buyer, with Bitcoin actually rallying on the news. The bigger move is on-chain. Robinhood's chain did $433 million of 24-hour DEX volume against Hyperliquid's $296 million and topped $1 billion in two days, with 194,000 daily active users, though 95 percent of that is still crypto-native and almost none of the 28 million Robinhood retail users have bridged over yet. And in a notable turn, JPMorgan walked back its Strategy warning and now says the real threat to Bitcoin is TradFi permissioned chains like its own Kinexys, not Strategy.

Psychology. Look at where the conviction is pooling this morning. Per the People list, the entire memory and semi complex is bid into the SK Hynix debut, Sandisk is up 800 percent on the year, Micron is being told it will out-earn its last 35 years combined, and Morgan Stanley is calling the power selloff a gift. Every one of those is a reason to be long, and they are all the same trade wearing different tickers. When a debut is oversubscribed seven times and the proxy and the leader and the analyst are all leaning the same direction, the crowd is not diversified, it is concentrated and telling itself a story about breadth.

Now hold the honest number next to the euphoria. Per the Financial Markets list, every Magnificent Seven name except Apple is already in a double-digit drawdown, Microsoft down 29 percent, while the index sits near a record because a handful of names carry it. That is the market quietly doing two things at once, celebrating at the index level and bleeding underneath. The discipline today is not to fight the Hynix trade, it is to size it like what it is. Ask whether you are buying memory because the setup is good or because everyone in your feed already owns it, and remember that the safest-feeling trade in the room is usually the most crowded one. If you need the debut to pop to feel right about the position, the position is already too big.
