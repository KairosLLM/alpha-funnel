Alphabet and Tesla report into tonight's close as the whole market holds one number in its head, and the day that led up to it was a memory-and-chip melt-up with Nvidia back over $5 trillion and Intel popping 9 percent

The tape flipped from deep red overnight to green, AMD agreed to put $5 billion into Anthropic and sell it two gigawatts of chips, and OpenAI lifted its compute budget to $750 billion. Oil broke $95, the 30-year Treasury yield held above 5 percent for the longest since 2007, and the bears are all pointing at the same thing, the debt underneath the build.

A Wednesday built entirely around the closing bell. The market spent the session buying the AI-infrastructure trade with both hands while a quieter group kept pointing at the leverage financing it. Here is the session recap, then five stories.

Session recap. The prints that define the quarter land as this publishes. Per the Stocks list, Alphabet was expected to report revenue near $117 billion and earnings around $3 a share with Google Cloud growth near 67 percent, and the line the market actually cares about is the capital-spending guide. Per the People list, Tesla was set to follow minutes later with consensus near $0.54 a share on $26 billion of revenue, a print the desk agrees is really about robotaxi guidance rather than the quarter itself.

The day into those prints was a melt-up in the exact names that got crushed. Per the Market list, the S&P and Nasdaq flipped from deep red overnight to green, Nvidia pushed back over $5 trillion and cleared $208 on the most bullish options flow of the day, and the memory and semiconductor complex ripped with Micron, SK Hynix, and Marvell all up sharply while Meta fell 9 percent over five days below its 200-day average. Per the Financial Markets list, Intel popped about 9 percent on a headcount-reduction announcement, gold broke $4,100, and the Atlanta Fed's year-ahead business inflation eased to 2.2 percent.

The macro backdrop stayed heavy. Per the Polymarket list, oil surged above $95 for the first time in six weeks and the 30-year Treasury yield held above 5 percent for the longest stretch since 2007. Per the Kalshi list, more than $300 billion was added to US market value in the last 50 minutes of a session that could not sit still ahead of the news.

1. Everything hinged on Alphabet's capex line, and the smart read was to fade the pop. The two prints carry the whole AI trade. Per the People list, the desk framed Alphabet at $347 and up 10 percent on the year with an implied move around 5 percent, and noted the big banks just showed what this market does to a good-but-not-perfect number, with Citi down 5 percent on a 45 percent profit jump. Per Milk Road, the bull case is simply that Google guides capital spending higher again and reignites the whole complex, and Gene Munster now expects hyperscaler capex growth near 37 percent starting with tonight.

The technicians were more skeptical of the first move. Per Stocks with Josh, Alphabet has been in a downtrend since losing $400 and needs to reclaim and hold $360, and while the stock will probably run up on the print, the higher-odds trade is to fade that pop into the seasonal weakness between August and October. His Tesla read is cleaner, that the stock needs to hold above $410 after earnings to start any real reversal, so the honest posture into the close is to let both numbers and both guides print before deciding the trade is fixed.

2. AMD put $5 billion into its own customer, and the bears say the whole build runs on debt. The deal of the day was a chipmaker financing its buyer. Per the People list, AMD agreed to invest up to $5 billion in Anthropic while selling it two gigawatts of MI450 chips from the first half of 2027, days after a similar Microsoft deal, and Lisa Su signaled AMD may even backstop Anthropic's data-center leases. Per the Kalshi list, OpenAI on the same day raised its projected cloud spending through 2030 to $750 billion.

That circularity is exactly what the skeptics are circling. Per Prof G Markets, Oracle is down 35 percent on the year with its credit cut to one rung above junk, it borrowed $43 billion for data centers against $67 billion of revenue while burning cash, Wisconsin just handed it a $7 billion collateral bill on a single gigawatt project, and $489 billion of AI-related debt has been issued this year with hyperscalers only 40 percent of it. Per the Stocks list, Bill Ackman put a number on the other side of it, estimating OpenAI loses about $14 billion this year on roughly $13 billion of revenue. When a chipmaker has to fund the customer who buys the chips, the demand is real but the financing is the fragile part.

3. The memory shortage is still the cleanest bull story, and photonics is the next chapter. The supply data keeps getting more extreme. Per Milk Road, SK Hynix DRAM export prices are up 527 percent year over year and roughly 20 percent month over month even as the stocks fell, 13 to 15 customers have locked in about 40 percent of SK Hynix's projected 2027 profit with next-year supply already sold out, and UBS models Micron generating more than $400 billion of cumulative free cash flow by 2028 at 85 percent gross margins and seven times forward earnings.

The trade after memory is light. Per Milk Road AI, the next leg is photonics as copper hits its limits, with Applied Optoelectronics growing its own lasers into a shortage and booking a $124 million single-month 800G order while demand runs past capacity into mid-2027, and Marvell as the connectivity layer that Wall Street sees nearly tripling revenue by 2029. Per the Stocks list, Applied Optoelectronics jumped 15 percent to around $119 on the day. These are the highest-beta way to own the buildout, so size them small, but the pricing power in memory is the part of this market with actual scarcity behind it.

4. The real bottleneck is power, and even Elon Musk is buying it with his own money. The constraint that everything else runs into is electricity. Per Limitless, Elon Musk personally spent about $1 billion to buy APR Energy for roughly a gigawatt of mobile gas-turbine power to feed Grok, surfaced only when an investor disclosed a $50 million return, and the reason is stark, that data-center demand has climbed toward 46 gigawatts while only about 5 gigawatts will actually come online this year against grid connections that take five to seven years.

The public ways to own the shortage are the power names. Per that same show, GE Vernova is the pick as the TSMC of power with orders booked through 2031 and a $7 billion Microsoft deal, alongside Bloom Energy and modular nuclear, while Oklo carries a 14-gigawatt pipeline against no operating reactor and no revenue until late 2027, per Rick Orford. And the way to own the machines that make the chips is ASML, per that channel, which controls all of EUV lithography with its high-NA tools at $400 million each and guides 2026 revenue toward $40 billion, a monopoly protected by physics that you wait to buy on the dip.

5. Crypto is arguing that the cycle low is already in, and the CLARITY Act just cleared committee. The corner that already took its pain is making a timing call. Per Crypto Currently, Bitcoin crossing 50 percent of supply in loss on June 5 is the same signal that fired at every prior major bottom, and 47 days later the low may already be behind it, though a break of the $50,000 realized price would break the thesis. Per CryptoQuant, Ethereum near $1,900 sits about 17 percent below its cost basis with two of five bottoming signals now bullish, and the desk reads Bitcoin's break above $66,000 as a bear-market rally on July seasonality rather than the all-clear.

The catalyst underneath it is legislative. Per the Stocks list, the CLARITY Act cleared the Senate Banking Committee 15 to 9 with Treasury Secretary Bessent calling it on the one-yard line, and Coinbase jumped 10 percent. Per Milk Road, the caution is that the market is buying Coinbase for the wrong reason, because clear rules also invite the banks in, and with 2026 passage still priced near 41 percent the pop gives itself back fast if the vote slips to next year.

Single names to track.

  • $AMD (Advanced Micro Devices). Agreed to invest up to $5 billion in Anthropic and sell it two gigawatts of MI450 chips from 2027, per the People list.
  • $AAOI (Applied Optoelectronics). Jumped 15 percent to about $119 on a $124 million single-month 800G order with demand past capacity into mid-2027, per the Stocks list.
  • $GEV (GE Vernova). Orders booked through 2031 and a $7 billion Microsoft deal as the cleanest way to own the power bottleneck, per Limitless.
  • $COIN (Coinbase). Up 10 percent on the CLARITY Act clearing committee, though 2026 passage is still priced near 41 percent, per Milk Road.

Psychology. Watch what the crowd did today before a single number printed. It bid Nvidia back over $5 trillion, ripped the memory names it had dumped a week ago, and pushed more than $300 billion into the tape in the final hour, all while retail kept adding and the bulls lined up behind one hope, that Alphabet guides capex higher and sends the whole complex flying. That is a market that has already decided the answer and is buying ahead of the proof.

The tell is who is standing on the other side and what they are looking at. Michael Burry is short Micron and Nvidia, Jeremy Grantham is calling for a 50 to 70 percent drawdown, and Prof G is walking through Oracle's junk-adjacent credit and the $489 billion of AI debt issued this year, and none of them are arguing the demand is fake. They are arguing the financing is, that AMD now has to fund the customer buying its chips and OpenAI loses $14 billion to earn $13 billion, a very different worry than a valuation being rich.

So hold the discipline the setup demands. The scarcity in memory and power is real and that is why the dip was bought, but a market this convinced of tonight's outcome is exactly the one that gaps the wrong way on a soft capex line or a hedged robotaxi answer. Let the guides print before you chase, own the shortage through the toolmakers and the power names the math supports, and keep enough dry powder that if the debt is the thing that breaks, you are the buyer and not the one being financed.
