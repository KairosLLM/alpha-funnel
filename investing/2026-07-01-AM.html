The memory trade ate the first half, and now Burry is shorting the melt-up into July

Stocks closed the best six months in years on a record-narrow semis bid, Michael Burry stacked shorts on Nvidia Tesla and Caterpillar, and Bitcoin lost its 200-week line as the forced-seller math around Saylor got real

MacroStocksBitcoinCrypto

Half the year is in the books and it was a monster. The Dow finished H1 up 8 percent, the S&P 500 up 8 percent, the Nasdaq up 11 percent, and the Russell 2000 up 21 percent for its best first half since 1991 (Financial Markets list). Q2 alone added roughly $8 trillion of market cap to the S&P, the best quarter since 2020 (Market list). Great number. Now read who carried it, because that is the whole story heading into July.

The only trade that mattered was memory, and that is both the bull case and the risk

One group did the work. H1's S&P leaderboard: $SNDK up 857 percent, $MU up 303 percent, $INTC up 278 percent, $WDC up 271 percent, $MRVL up 250 percent, $DELL up 243 percent, $AMD up 171 percent (Financial Markets list). The semis index $SOX ran 83 percent in Q2 against 9 percent for the Mag 7, a full rotation out of the mega-cap generals and into the picks-and-shovels (People to follow list). Three of the new members of the $1 trillion club this quarter were memory names. Semiconductors are now a record 19.7 percent of the entire S&P 500 (Polymarket list).

That is roughly a fifth of the index riding on one theme. Jim Cramer is out saying $MU "tells its story better" than $NVDA, and Traderstewie has $MU as his IBD Top 50 number one with a $1,400 to $1,500 target (Market list). When the leadership is this narrow and this loved, you own it and you respect it at the same time. SK Hynix just filed to list in the US under $SKHY, one more sign of how crowded this memory trade has gotten.

Michael Burry just shorted the melt-up

Michael Burry is now short $NVDA, $TSLA, and $CAT, the first time he has been on Caterpillar, confirmed across multiple lists (Market list). Burry is early and often wrong on timing, so this is not a sell signal by itself. But shorting Nvidia, Tesla, and the single most cyclical industrial name in the market at once is a coherent bet that the melt-up is late-cycle. Pair it with the accounting story running underneath the tape. A University of Florida study says roughly 12 percent of Q1 S&P earnings were unrealized markups on OpenAI and Anthropic stakes rather than real operating profit (Prof G Markets). Strip the paper gains and earnings growth falls back to the five-year average. The melt-up is real. The quality underneath it is thinner than the tape suggests.

Bitcoin broke, and the Saylor math is the story now

Crypto is the opposite picture and it is ugly. Bitcoin closed below its 200-week moving average (the four-year trend line that has floored past bear markets) for the first time since 2023, down about 20 percent in June and 53 percent from its all-time high, trading in the high $50Ks to low $60Ks (Kalshi list). Whales are pressing it. On Hyperliquid there are three separate leveraged shorts worth more than $100 million combined, including a 20x position of 1,100 BTC with a liquidation up at $67,914 (Hermes list).

The bottom-callers cluster lower. Trader Mayne wants $49,500 to $50,000 first and would not be shocked by a flush to $38,000 to $40,000 into Q4, and he is only a buyer heavy in the high $30Ks to high $40Ks (Trader Mayne). His cycle model caps the next bull run near $180,000, not the $500,000 everyone spent last year screaming about. Kaleo says low 50s into the 40s. Polymarket treats sub-$50K as almost a certainty.

The variable that could actually mark the low is Michael Saylor. Strategy ($MSTR) is now something like $14 to $16 billion underwater on its stack with an average cost far above spot, and it owes roughly $1.7 billion a year in perpetual preferred dividends it can pause but not stop (threadguy). Kaleo is arguing Saylor should proactively sell $1.25 billion of BTC now (Hermes list). If a new monetization program forces Saylor to actually sell Bitcoin to fund those dividends, that is the FTX-style forced-seller capitulation that tends to print the bottom. Watch it.

The bull who is buying the knife

The other side has a name and a balance sheet. Ben Werkman, CIO of Strive ($ASST, the Vivek Ramaswamy-founded treasury firm run by ex-CalPERS bond chief Matt Cole), is buying, close to 20,000 BTC on the books after picking up 2,500 then 1,500 coins in the $60Ks (Milk Road). His pitch is the 200-week and power-law floor as an entry, fiat debasement at 6.7 percent M2 growth, and a sovereign bearer-asset thesis. Strive's preferred $SATA pays a 13 percent dividend daily and trades around $90 against $100 par, and Werkman blames the discount on a leverage unwind in Strategy's STRC paper, not credit. He held an 18-month dividend reserve and sat out last week's mess. If you want the bull case articulated by someone with skin in it, that is it.

Names on the tape worth a look

  • $UWMC (UWM Holdings) is down about 80 percent from its 2024 high to around $2, at capitulation volume, and tomorrow July 2 there is a Two Harbors merger vote that would improve its leverage (r/UWMC). It holds 44 percent wholesale market share and originates 10 to 12 percent of all US mortgages, trading at a base-case P/E under 5. The risk is CEO Mat Ishbia, who pledged more than half his stake (805 million shares) to JPMorgan and has pulled $4.4 billion out of the company since 2021. He also stopped selling, last hitting the tape at $3.39 in May.
  • $NOK (Nokia) is a stealth AI-networking play. Nvidia put $1 billion into it, it bought Infinera for optical, and connecting tens of thousands of GPUs is exactly its lane (r/Nokia).
  • $RKLB (Rocket Lab) is acquiring Iridium ($IRDM) in a roughly $8 billion deal (Market list). $AMBA popped 22 percent on edge-AI silicon. $NBIS ran 175 percent in Q2. $EOSE (Eos Energy, zinc-based long-duration storage) is moving from proving the tech to proving management can execute (r/Eos). $IONQ stays the leading pure-play quantum bet with the valuation priced for perfection (Rick Orford).
  • $SOL has two independent bulls. Market Bubble sees a run from $60 to roughly $600 this cycle on a break of the prior all-time high (Market Bubble), and Trader Mayne is long from about $67. ETH at roughly $1,570 gets a $6,000 fair-value case from DeFi Dad, who values it as the cost to attack the network securing $160 billion in stablecoins and $200 billion in DeFi (DeFi Dad).

Market context: what to watch into the open

Q3 opens on the back of the best first half since the early 90s, and July is historically kind. Ryan Detrick notes the S&P has been higher in July 11 straight years for a 2.9 percent average, and 5 for 5 under Trump (Financial Markets list). The Dow closed above 52,000 for the first time. The catalyst that matters this week is Thursday's jobs report, and after JOLTS openings ran hot at 7.594 million versus 7.296 million expected and Fed's Hammack called the labor market "right around full employment," a softer payrolls print is the good outcome that eases the hawkish worry (People to follow list). Also on the radar: the Two Harbors and $UWMC vote tomorrow, whether the SK Hynix $SKHY IPO adds fuel to the memory bid, and the reaction function under new Fed Chair Kevin Warsh. Key level to hold is SPY 743. The tape is strong, the leadership is narrow, and the smartest bear in the room just shorted three of the biggest names at once. I stay long the memory trade and keep the stop tight.
