Iran says the strait is closed, Trump says it is open, and oil already gapped, so the calm trade meets its first real bill Monday

Brent settled near $76 after a 5 percent week and ran further on the weekend tape while crossings fell to a tenth of normal, and futures are called moderately red into a loaded week. Underneath the war, the AI-power supply chain got a concrete map, the memory trade heads into its biggest catalyst week yet, and the market started asking whether the AI spend actually pays.

MacroStocksCrypto

Market's closed, so this is the setup for a week that arrives with a live oil shock and a full catalyst calendar on top of it. The weekend did not settle the war, it escalated it, and it handed the tape a real gap to open into. Here is the week ahead, then five stories.

The week ahead. The overnight fact is a standoff over the Strait of Hormuz. Iran's Revolutionary Guard says it is closed until further notice, and on Meet the Press Trump countered that the strait is open to commercial shipping, with the US military saying it is positioned to keep it open, per the Kalshi and Market lists. The tape is not waiting for the words to agree, Brent settled near $76 after a 5 percent week and ran about 3 percent further on the weekend 24-hour venues, per the Crypto list, while commercial crossings fell to roughly 11 a day, about a tenth of the normal run rate.

The rest of the week is heavy on its own. SK Hynix converts to regular-way $SKHY trading Monday, the $SKHX 2x long and $SKHZ short ETFs launch Tuesday alongside June CPI (the inflation print that settles the July 29 Fed debate), $NFLX and $ASML report, and Tesla reports July 22. Futures are called moderately red into Monday absent a weekend peace headline, per the Market AM list, so respect the gap rather than fading it blind.

1. The oil shock is real but the structural bears are already leaning on it. The bull read is simple, a fifth of the world's seaborne oil moves through Hormuz and the throughput just collapsed, so any sustained closure gaps crude and everything levered to it. The counter is that Trump and CENTCOM are publicly calling the strait open, and Mike McGlone's commodity framing on the Financial Markets AM list puts Brent resistance near $83 against a base case that rotates back toward $40 as US and Canada near a 9-million-barrel-a-day surplus by 2027.

So the trade is a spike to fade rather than a new bull market in oil, and the refiners that already ran, $VLO and $MPC at 52-week highs per the Financial Markets PM list, are the crowded expression. Watch Monday's open for whether the gap holds past the first hour or fills.

2. The memory trade heads into its biggest catalyst week with a mechanical bull case underneath it. The $DRAM memory ETF crossed $25 billion in assets for the first time ever, per the Kalshi AM list, and the SK Hynix listing mechanics are the story into Monday. Per the Stocks PM list, the roughly $26 to $28 billion ADR was more than seven times oversubscribed and funds sold $MU, $SIVE, and $AAOI to fund it, so the midweek Micron wobble reads as a rebalance rather than a thesis break.

The conviction is getting stretched in a way worth naming. Dylan Patel now floats $MU at $4,000 on memory capacity growing only 20 to 30 percent a year against demand that doubles, per the Stocks AM list, and Jordi Visser points at Samsung trading near 4 times earnings on a 19-fold profit jump as the cheap way in, per his long market read. The oligopoly math is unchanged, Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron hold about 90 percent of DRAM through 2027. The mechanical rebound is a real trade, the $4,000 target is a story you do not need to own the stock, and the honest move is to keep those two separate.

3. The AI-power trade finally has a named supply chain, not just a slogan. The most useful new map of the week, per Dylan Patel's breakdown carried by Melvin: AI racks are moving from 48-volt to 800-volt direct-current power because the current at a megawatt rack would melt copper otherwise, and that transition creates four investable layers. The first is power semiconductors made of silicon carbide and gallium nitride replacing silicon, with Infineon estimating about $175,000 of chip content per megawatt of rack power against nearly zero today, and the names are $IFNNY, $ON, $WOLF, $NVTS, and $STM.

The other three layers are just as concrete. Power conversion runs through $VRT (Vertiv, Nvidia's lead 800-volt architectural partner), $ETN, and $MPWR. The grid-to-site gear is $GEV (GE Vernova), whose orders are running at twice its shipment rate, a forward-demand tell. And behind-the-meter generation, bypassing the five-year grid-connection queue, is $BE (Bloom Energy). The number that frames the whole trade, per the same thread, is that solar plus four hours of battery now costs $57 a megawatt-hour against $102 for new gas, so the power gets built wherever it is cheapest and fastest.

4. The market started asking the uncomfortable question: does the AI spend actually pay. The bill-shock stories piled up this weekend. Joseph Hogue's seven-stock breakdown cites a Fortune 500 firm overspending $500 million in a single month, Meta burning 60 trillion tokens in 30 days, and Uber blowing its annual AI budget in three months, and points the trade at the cost-control layer, $NET (Cloudflare, AI gateway), $NOW (ServiceNow, agent orchestration), $DDOG (Datadog, agent observability), and $PLTR. The counterweight is real, a PwC survey found 56 percent of CEOs report no revenue or cost benefit from AI yet, the "pilot purgatory" Chamath flagged on the Crypto PM list.

The bull rebuttal is that the spend is real adoption rather than waste. Per Visser's read, SemiAnalysis's own AI bill went from under $100,000 to about $11 million annualized in six months once Claude Code became usable, and his frame for the whole tape is to short abundance (anything a computer can replicate) and stay long scarcity (memory, compute, power). The drawdown, he argues, was a speed crash, a sentiment cleanse, not the top. This is the debate that decides the second half, and both sides now have real numbers.

5. Amazon's custom silicon quietly became a $20 billion business, and the neocloud moat got more expensive. Per Rick Orford, Amazon's custom-chip business (Trainium, Graviton, and Nitro) hit a $20 billion annualized run rate in the first quarter on triple-digit growth, Trainium 3 is nearly sold out, and Anthropic has committed up to 5 gigawatts and OpenAI about 2 gigawatts of it through AWS. That is Amazon building a real second source to Nvidia inside its own cloud, and $MRVL rides it on a multi-year supply deal.

The neocloud side got a moat update from the Milk Road weekend note: the cost to build a gigawatt of compute jumped from $50 billion to $100 billion in about three months, and Nvidia is backstopping the GPU financing for the neoclouds, acting as landlord and bank at once. Melvin's blunt version is that if you are not buying $NBIS (Nebius) or $CRWV (CoreWeave), you are missing the names with locked capacity nobody can afford to replicate.

Single names worth your watchlist.

  • $SOFI (SoFi). Per Invest with Henry, CEO Anthony Noto has bought roughly 130,000 shares this year at an average near $17.29, about $2.5 million of his own money, with the stock at $18.78 and Q1 revenue up 41 percent to $1.1 billion. Still below the $20 line that turns the chart.
  • $NOW (ServiceNow). Per Stocks with Josh, a trade setup off a 36 percent drawdown with Nvidia publicly endorsing the partnership, a 95 to 110 near-term range, a stop at 87.50, and 136 as the upside target.
  • $TSLA (Tesla). Into the July 22 print, June saw Model Y take the top sales spot across China, the UK, Switzerland, and Australia, and Japan up 183 percent year over year, while the AI5 chip reached tape-out on Samsung's 2-nanometer Texas line, per the People PM list.
  • $GLXY (Galaxy Digital). Per the Crypto PM list, roughly $25 against a $38 to $41 fair value on the Helios data-center contract with CoreWeave, with a refinancing and a second tenant as catalysts.
  • $BABA (Alibaba). Per Hogue, the open-source play, with Qwen past 700 million downloads and up about 20 percent in three weeks, as Chinese models pressure US labs on price.
  • Bitcoin. Standard Chartered reiterated $100,000 by December but buried a warning that below $60,000 the selling pressure builds, with $57,000 as the level that held as the cycle low, per Lark Davis. Novogratz frames a $65,000 to $85,000 range with $60,000 as the line that decides it, per the Crypto PM list.

Psychology. The tell this weekend is a market that spent a week congratulating itself for not caring, walking straight into the first thing it actually has to price. Bitcoin fell a few hundred dollars on Iran declaring the strait closed and recovered half of it within an hour, per the Crypto AM list, and the loudest voices on the memory trade are posting $4,000 Micron targets and a record $25 billion in the DRAM ETF at the exact moment the group heads into a five-catalyst week. That is what late-cycle confidence looks like, a crowd so well trained to buy the dip that it stops checking whether this dip is different.

The countercurrent worth respecting is that the people who moved early are getting cautious rather than bolder. Michael Saylor sold about 3,588 Bitcoin, his largest since 2020, Jordi Visser sold his Micron in mid-May well before the top and only plans to buy it back, and the smart-money power trade is rotating into unglamorous names like Vertiv and GE Vernova rather than adding to the leaders. When the early money is trimming winners and the late money is raising targets, the tape is telling you which end of the trade you are standing on.

So the discipline check going into Monday is about the gap. If you are tempted to buy the memory names or Tesla on the open because every dip this month has paid, remember you would be buying the most crowded book in years into an oil shock the market has not settled and a CPI print two days out. Size for the gap, keep the levels in front of you, $60,000 on Bitcoin, $20 on SoFi, Brent's $83, and let Monday show you whether the crowd was right to stop caring or just lucky until now.
