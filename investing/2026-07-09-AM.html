Iran hit US bases overnight, the US struck back, and then one 1am Trump tweet that Iran wants a deal flipped the whole selloff green

The market is so tired of this war it bought the dip before dawn, the Fed minutes said nobody wants to hike until 2027, and the entire tape now hinges on memory as SK Hynix lists Friday into a 7x oversubscribed book. Here is the open.

StocksMacroBitcoinCrypto

The overnight tape was a war movie with a happy ending nobody quite believes. Iran fired missiles at US bases, the US hit back with a second round, and then a single pre-dawn line from Trump that Iran wants to negotiate erased the entire risk-off move. Underneath, the Fed took hikes off the table and the whole market reorganized itself around one question: did memory just bottom. Here is the open, then the six things that matter.

The open. Whippy but net green. Per the Market list, overnight the US launched a second, larger round of strikes on Iranian targets around the Strait of Hormuz while Iran fired missiles at US bases in Kuwait, Bahrain, and Qatar, and then at roughly 1:15am Trump said Iran called and wants a deal, which turned Nasdaq futures from deep red to green. Brent is still up about 2% above $75, and one trader summed up the mood as the market having priced the war in both directions.

The Fed gave the bulls cover. Per ClearValue Tax, the June FOMC minutes showed a unanimous hold with not a single official wanting a hike, the internal survey does not pencil a first cut until the second quarter of 2027, and after the release the odds of a July hike fell to about 30%. Memory is the fulcrum today, with the Kospi down 25% from its high even as SK Hynix lists Friday into a book 7x oversubscribed.

1. The war became a buy-the-dip in a single overnight tweet. This is the tell about where sentiment actually is. Per the Market list, a genuine escalation, Iran striking US bases and the US answering, got fully faded before the US open the moment Trump floated a deal, with traders openly saying the market stops going lower on bad news and the bottom is near. Per threadguy, this is the first war people got bored of, the tape has simply stopped trading it. That is either resilience or complacency, and the two look identical until they do not.

2. The Fed minutes said nobody wants to hike, and the first cut is a year away. The macro anchor is a Fed with no plan. Per ClearValue Tax, every participant backed holding in June, a few saw a case for raising but still voted to hold, and the dots split on the year with many seeing rates roughly where they are and many others seeing them higher. The honest read is that inflation is running hotter on tariffs, energy, and, notably, demand from the AI buildout, which the Fed itself flagged as showing no signs of slowing. A reactive Fed that will not hike but cannot cut is a green light with a short leash.

3. Memory is the whole market, and Friday is the referendum. Everything routes through one sector. Per the Financial Markets list, SK Hynix lists Friday with roughly a 48% operating margin against Micron's 26%, and per the People-to-follow list the deal raises about $24.5B, the second-largest US debut ever, trading when-issued Friday under SKHYV with Baillie Gifford and Coatue indicated up to $7B. Penguin Solutions $PENG ripped more than 20% on a memory guidance raise, now up 293% since Trump's March buy.

The bull case on Micron got concrete. Per Rick Orford and a detailed Reddit DCF, $MU trades at 9.2 times earnings with a data-center run rate over $100B, is practically sold out through 2027, has locked 16 strategic customer agreements worth $20B of recurring revenue, and models out anywhere from $900 to $1,550 fair value. Per the Stocks list, the chart is holding the lower bound of its uptrend channel with 971 the level that confirms the reversal. The bear is not fundamentals, it is commoditization. Per threadguy, the risk is China's CXMT selling cheap DRAM into the US so buyers like Apple are no longer pigeonholed into Micron and SanDisk. Friday decides which story the tape believes.

4. Nvidia is sitting on the line, and it is the cheapest it has been in years. The single most important chart bounced. Per the Market list, $NVDA bounced twice off its 200-day near $190, trades at 16 times 2027 earnings against an average target near $317, and per the Kalshi list the whole Magnificent Seven is now the cheapest it has been versus the S&P in over a decade, with tech insiders buying at a record pace.

The bears are not gone. Per Stocks with Josh, the trend is still down with lower highs and lower lows, and he sees real support only at 172 and the range neckline at 165, guessing Michael Burry likely covered his short in that zone. Hold the 200-day and the cheapest Nvidia in years is a gift. Lose it and 165 is the next stop.

5. Bitcoin's bottom keeps building, and the next buyer is not Saylor. The structure improved again. Per the Crypto list, reactive speculators now hold just 26% of Bitcoin's realized cap, below the 40% line that has marked every major bottom, and Michael Saylor's record sale was absorbed with the coin barely dipping. Per Milk Road and Bitwise, the thesis is that Strategy is no longer the marginal buyer and the baton is passing to institutions, with Vanguard's $11T hiring of a head of digital assets read as the same one-way door BlackRock walked through.

The skeptics have a point too. Per the Crypto list, Peter Schiff is hammering that the biggest buyer became a seller and the coattail trade flipped, while Lyn Alden's base case is no new highs this year but no new lows either. Bitcoin is chopping around $60,000 to $62,000, a contested bottom, not a confirmed turn.

6. Under all the bottom talk, the memecoin casino says the froth has not left. Respect this counter-signal. Per threadguy, two memecoins on the new Robinhood Chain, Cash Cat and Anom, ran to nine-figure market caps in a week after Vlad Tenev followed the account, all while real on-chain volume sits at all-time lows. Per Capital Flows, the money that is not gambling is chasing Hyperliquid treasuries like $PURR instead. Nine-figure gambling on dead-quiet volume is the sound a market makes late, not early.

Single names worth your watchlist.

  • $SKHY (SK Hynix). The Nasdaq debut prices Friday, 7x oversubscribed and the second-largest US listing ever, trading when-issued under SKHYV with 2x-long $SKHX and 1x-short $SKHZ ETFs following Monday. The purest read on whether the memory trade bottomed, and the cleanest way to trade it besides Micron.
  • $PENG (Penguin Solutions). Per the Market list, a small-cap memory name up more than 20% on a roughly $100M guidance raise and 293% since March. High beta on the same theme, so size it like one.
  • $GE (GE Aerospace). Per one Reddit teardown, a 45% ROE quality business whose revenue grew 24.7% while EPS fell 1.8%, trading at nearly 51 times forward earnings against a six-model fair value of $119 that pegs it 67% overpriced. A great company at a price with no floor if the earnings dip is not transitory.
  • $ETN (Eaton). Per Rick Orford, the electrical-equipment maker framed as the AI-power analog to Nvidia, sitting inside the electrification and data-center demand mismatch. A power-infrastructure compounder, though valuation still has to work.
  • $PFE (Pfizer). Per Stocks with Josh, the stated buy is $23 for a 6 to 7% yield, with August 4 earnings and a history of running up about 20 days prior, which points at roughly July 16.

Psychology. Look at what the crowd decided overnight. Iran hit American bases, the US struck back, and the market bought the dip before sunrise on a single tweet that a deal might happen, because it is, in one trader's words, bored of the war. At the same time memory fell 25% in Korea and the response is to call a bottom, pile into a 7x-oversubscribed IPO, and buy Nvidia because it is the cheapest since 2019, all while insiders buy at a record pace. Everyone wants this to be the bottom, and that unanimity is the actual risk. When war is a buy, every dip is a floor, and the IPO buyers and the insiders and the bottom-callers are all leaning the same way, the consensus that it is over becomes the crowded trade.

Now hold the counter-signal next to it. Per threadguy, two memecoins ran to nine figures on record-low volume this week, and that is late-cycle gambling energy, not the fear you get at a real bottom. And per the Crypto list, Bitcoin is doing the honest version of a bottom, the fast money gone, Saylor finished buying, everyone waiting for $40,000, and it simply refuses to break. The discipline today is to notice that the loudest conviction is on the long side of the same three trades, and that the asset with no fans left is the one quietly holding. Feelings are cheapest exactly where nobody wants to feel them.
