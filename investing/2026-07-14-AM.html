CPI lands this morning and the leveraged SK Hynix ETFs launch into a name already down 38 percent, so the crowd gets a bigger lever right as the pros reach for cash

The trading desks are rotating out of crypto and into crude, Benjamin Cowen says Bitcoin's real bottom is late September rather than December, and the structural bull case for US assets got a fresh catalyst date. Meanwhile Robinhood Chain keeps flipping Hyperliquid on volume without taking its moat.

MacroBitcoinCrypto

Two events define the session before it opens. June CPI prints this morning, and the two leveraged SK Hynix ETFs go live the same day the underlying is already 38 percent off its high. Here is the pre-open picture, then four stories.

Pre-open preview. The calendar is the catalyst. June CPI (the inflation print that settles the July 29 Fed debate) lands this morning, and a bullish weekly close usually has its low in by Wednesday, so today and tomorrow set the week's tone, per the trading desk at Cookbook. The same morning brings a 2x long and a 1x short SK Hynix ETF, arriving after the stock fell 15 percent Monday to 38 percent below its June high, with technical gap-fills sitting at 1,680 and then 1,440 that would wipe out leveraged holders, per threadguy's recap.

The one floor under it is Leopold Aschenbrenner, reportedly bidding $7 to $10 billion for the US listing at a 10 percent premium. Bitcoin sits around $61,000, and the levels to watch are $60,000 to $62,000 as the week's low and $64,500 to $65,000 as the reclaim that changes the picture.

1. The clearest voices on Bitcoin agree the real bottom is still weeks away. Benjamin Cowen's structural read is that 2026 is a lower-volatility carbon copy of the 2018 bear market, with Bitcoin squeezed between its 200-week moving average (the long-run trend line) and the bear-market resistance band above. His fractal has the dip into CPI happening now, a bounce, then the gains given back by mid-to-late August, the same round-trip 2018 and 2022 both ran. His cycle-bottom call is late September or early October, and he is blunt that waiting for a December low is greedy.

The trading desk arrives at the same place from the tape. Per Cookbook, the levels converge on $60,000 to $62,000 as the week's low and a reclaim of $64,500 to $65,000 as the trigger to get interested, with one trader holding a partial long from $58,000 hedged with a short from the $64,000s. The shared warning is about altcoins. Cowen's point, made for the twentieth time, is that even if Bitcoin holds its line, the alts bleed through August and September the way they did in 2018 while Bitcoin sat at $6,000, because the business cycle does not support a rotation into them.

2. The rotation the desks are actually making is out of crypto and into crude. The live tactical trade, per Charlie at Cookbook: long crude oil with a wide stop, on the view that the Strait of Hormuz conflict produces a bouncing ball of diminishing price spikes rather than one clean panic. He closed his Hyperliquid position for a loss when the thesis broke, called the ETH/BTC ratio done, and is pivoting toward traditional markets on the blunt logic that the opportunities in crypto are getting smaller by the day.

That squares with the oddity of the oil tape. Five months into the conflict, with Trump now taxing cargo through the strait, crude is only back to roughly where it traded on March 1, per threadguy. The crude trade is not a bet that oil explodes, it is a bet that a market this calm about an active war is underpricing the tail, and that the next spike finds a buyer. It is the cleaner expression of the same caution driving money out of the alts.

3. The structural bull case for US assets got a fresh catalyst date. Underneath the daily chop is the reason US valuations stay elevated, laid out by Capital Flows: China runs roughly a $1 trillion goods surplus behind a closed capital account, so that money has nowhere to go but into US assets, and that forced recycling rather than sentiment is why the S&P has held rich multiples for a decade. The dollar is 58 percent of world reserves and 88 percent of currency trades, and BowTied Bull adds that foreigners own about 21.4 percent of US stocks and a quarter of US bonds, while Trump Accounts are quietly turning the S&P 500 into the country's default retirement bid.

The actionable piece is a date. Per Capital Flows, the CME launches single-stock futures on Nvidia, AMD, and other tech names on July 27, which are the pre-staged instruments for the one tail that breaks this whole structure: a move on Taiwan. TSMC is the single choke point of the AI arms race, so a Taiwan-invasion headline is a same-instant signal to short the chips, and now there will be a clean way to do it. The same note argues gold does not revisit $3,000, because its rally was a hedge on the trade system itself rather than a bet on dollar devaluation.

4. Robinhood Chain keeps flipping Hyperliquid on volume, but the moat has not moved. The nuance worth holding, per threadguy: Robinhood Chain has now beaten Hyperliquid in 24-hour decentralized-exchange volume three days running at roughly $1 billion a day, a week after launch, with 17 million transactions, 350,000 addresses, and $250 million in deposits. That is real adoption, and $HOOD has decoupled from Bitcoin, up 80 percent off its May lows. But his verdict on the actual competition is that Hyperliquid keeps its trader-and-liquidity moat, because the depth that serious traders need is too established for a brokerage chain to pull away, at least yet. The trade is long the Robinhood story and skeptical of the Hyperliquid obituary at the same time.

Single names worth your watchlist.

  • Crude oil. The desks' rotation trade, long with a wide stop on the view that a calm war tape underprices the next Hormuz spike, per Cookbook.
  • $HOOD (Robinhood). Decoupled from Bitcoin and up 80 percent off the May lows as its chain flips Hyperliquid on volume, though the trader moat holds, per threadguy.
  • $NVDA / $AMD. The CME lists single-stock futures on both July 27, the clean hedge instrument for a Taiwan-TSMC shock, per Capital Flows.
  • $SKHY (SK Hynix). The 2x long and 1x short ETFs launch today into a stock 38 percent off its high, with gap-fills at 1,680 and 1,440 and Aschenbrenner reportedly bidding the ADR at a premium, per threadguy.
  • Bitcoin. Squeezed between the 200-week average and resistance, with a cycle bottom Cowen puts in late September, and $60,000 to $65,000 the range that decides the week, per Cowen.

Psychology. The most honest sentence in the whole batch came from a trader describing his own rules, not the market. Round-tripping sucks, Magus wrote on the Cookbook desk, so he would rather sell too early than watch a gain turn into a loss, and Charlie backed it up by closing his Hyperliquid trade for a loss the moment the thesis broke rather than hoping it back. That is what discipline sounds like from people who do this for a living, and it is the opposite of what today's product launch invites.

Because the other thing happening this morning is a 2x SK Hynix ETF going live so the crowd can bet bigger on a name already down 38 percent, right as Aschenbrenner tries to catch it and Cowen warns the real bottom is two months out. The tape is handing retail a bigger lever at the exact moment the professionals are shortening their own. When the pros are rotating to cash and crude and selling their crypto early, and the new toy is leverage on the thing that just cracked, the crowded side of the boat is obvious.

So the discipline check into CPI is to notice which trade you are being sold. Buying the 2x SK Hynix launch or catching the memory knife is the leveraged-Korean trade Cowen and the desks are stepping away from. If you want to be positioned, the desks are telling you where they went, into crude with a wide stop and into cash, waiting for Bitcoin to reclaim $65,000 or for the September low to actually arrive. Let the print come first, and let the level prove itself before you add.
