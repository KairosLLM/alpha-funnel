The memory trade cracked and Meta became a cloud company in one afternoon

Chips got dumped while $META ripped 11% on a compute pivot, and Bitcoin printed a fresh low under $58K right on Ben Cowen's 2018 script.

MacroStocksBitcoinCrypto

Today was the rotation, live on the tape. If you have been waiting to see what "the leaders get caught offside" actually looks like, you just watched it happen in real time.

The close. Chips got taken to the woodshed while everything else held up. The semis led the drop, with the $SOX (the Philadelphia semiconductor index) off as much as 6.7% intraday:

  • Chip decliners: $MU down 6.7%, $ARM off 4.6%, $INTC down 4%, $AMD down 3.5%, $TSM off 3.4%, and even $NVDA down 2.3%.
  • The other side of the boat: mega-caps went green into the bell, $MSFT, $AAPL, $TSLA, $AMZN, and $GOOGL all near highs of day, and software ($IGV) up 4%.

Charlie Bilello's stat of the day: semis are up 237% in fourteen months, which now exceeds the dot-com-peak surge.

Under the tape it was a disinflation print. ISM Manufacturing landed at 53.3 (below the 54.0 expected but a sixth straight month of expansion), and prices-paid collapsed from 82.1 to 73.0, the biggest four-month drop in four years. ADP private payrolls came in soft at 98k against 118k expected. WTI crude slid under $68.50, a four-month low, with the entire Iran-war premium now gone.

Heads up on the calendar: US markets are closed Friday July 3 for Independence Day, so the real catalyst is Thursday's jobs report, pulled forward a day.

1. The memory air pocket showed up. For weeks the loudest trade in this book has been memory, and today it finally coughed. $MU gave back 6.7% on a day the whole complex bled. Do not confuse a shakeout with a top. The bull case is still intact and it is a good one: on Prof G Markets, Bernstein's Stacy Rasgon put roughly 70% of the semis rally down to actual earnings growth, not multiple expansion, and laid out why memory is the real bottleneck. HBM (high-bandwidth memory, the stacked chips that feed AI accelerators) eats four times the wafer capacity of standard DDR5, so adding wafers does not add bits, and clean-room shortage caps supply into 2027 and beyond. His read is the upcycle has duration and the "air pocket" is a 2028 problem, not a this-year problem. Milk Road Macro reminded everyone how far this has run: $MU +150%, Nebius $NBIS +124%, Hyperliquid $HYPE +114%, Bloom Energy $BE +110%, SK Square $SKSQ +63% over the stretch. When a trade is up like that and everyone can recite the thesis, days like today are the tax you pay. I am watching $MU into the high-70s gross-margin story, not selling the shakeout.

2. Meta became a cloud company, or the market decided it did. The single biggest move today was $META up 10 to 11% on "Meta Compute," reporting that it will sell its excess AI capacity AWS-Bedrock style and rent raw compute like a neocloud. Jim Cramer slapped a "$100 a share" on it. The bull frame is a capex-to-revenue bridge on a name trading around 9x earnings near a two-and-a-half-year low versus the $SPX. Here is the tell I would not ignore, flagged across the Financial Markets list by Jeff Pu and others: Meta just signed a $21B CoreWeave deal and Google is throttling Gemini compute, which makes Meta look compute-constrained, not oversupplied. So which is it, excess capacity to sell or a shortage to cover? You cannot be both. The neoclouds voted on the read: $CRWV fell 9.7 to 12% and $NBIS dropped 13% on the same headline. On the Market list, Amit Kukreja bought that $NBIS dip at $232.49 and called it an overreaction, noting AWS just raised GPU rents 20%. Reddit had the same instinct earlier, framing it as Zuck choosing violence against the neoclouds. My take: the pop is real, the "$100" is a meme, and the CoreWeave contract is the fact that has to be reconciled before I trust the excess-capacity story.

3. Bitcoin printed the low, right on schedule. $BTC made a fresh cycle low near $57,700 today, and two independent chartists I track called the same thing without talking to each other. Benjamin Cowen says 2026 is tracking 2018 almost tick for tick, the June 2018 low was $5,743 and today we tapped ~$5,770-scaled at $57.7K, hence his "simulation confirmed." His plan is unglamorous: ignore Bitcoin in the first half of a midterm year, DCA through the second half, and assume the bottom lands somewhere between $30K and $60K. Crypto Currently is looking at the same chart from the bull side: price is below the 200-week moving average (a once-every-four-years event), and for the first time since the 2022 bottom the weekly RSI printed a bullish divergence (lower low in price, higher low in momentum). Realized price (the average price at which every coin last moved, a proxy for the network's aggregate cost basis) sits at $53,500, and Tether ($USDT) just flipped Ethereum ($ETH) by market cap for the first time ever, which is the kind of thing that happens at washouts. The flows back the fear: Degen Sensei notes US spot BTC ETFs just booked a record $4.5B of June outflows, the worst month on record. Kalshi has only 12% odds Bitcoin hits $100K this year (an all-time low) and 70% odds it trades below $50K. Both creators are DCA-buying under $60K and neither is going all in. I am doing the same. This looks like an oversold bounce setup, not a confirmed trend reversal. Watch for a weekly higher low to confirm a July relief rally into the 200-day, and watch ETF outflows flipping positive as the real bottom signal.

4. Solana is the relative-strength name in crypto. If you want the coin that is acting best, it is $SOL, holding around $77 to $79 while Bitcoin makes new lows. Lark Davis gives the actionable level: a daily close at or above $76 opens a run at $97, roughly 28%. Two well-known traders (Psycho, who publicly bought the $15 Solana bottom last cycle, and OSF) are both accumulating spot here and calling it a "lock in now" zone. Just respect that if Bitcoin pukes again, Solana rolls over with it, so you are buying leveraged beta on a Bitcoin bounce here.

5. Tomorrow is a Tesla day. $TSLA reports Q2 deliveries pre-market Thursday July 2, and the gap between estimates is enormous: the Street is around 406k, Troy Teslike is at 466k. The stock is up 16% since Friday and pinned near $400, which happens to be the level where Michael Burry's short sits on the wrong side. Reclaim it cleanly and the momentum crowd targets $440 then $520. This is a binary, so size it like one. The read-through worth noting: $GM's US EV sales fell 41% year over year in Q2, so the EV pie is not the story, Tesla's own number is.

Single names worth your watchlist. A few high-conviction ideas landed today that fit a position-builder better than the day's noise:

  • The BEDS trade ($GEO + $CXW). The detention-bed thesis is the cleanest catalyst setup I saw. Federal agencies need roughly ten turnkey facilities, GEO Group and CoreCivic each sit on 20,000-plus idle beds, and both trade at $3 to $4B caps against a $70B funding backdrop. $CXW around $30.50 and $GEO around $29.50, both already up 30 to 45% since June. Benchmark puts 80% odds on a two-facility sale with price targets to $36, Northland sees $40 on both. Both just beat on EPS and raised guidance. Call-to-put volume is running 30-to-1. Risk is a policy-shutdown headline, which knocked them 20% earlier this year.
  • BlackBerry ($BB). The least-covered name with the strongest fundamental turn. QNX is the safety-certified OS in 275 million-plus vehicles and the foundation under NVIDIA's DRIVE and Thor platforms. Revenue up 26%, adjusted EBITDA up 144%, and the first positive operating cash flow in a first quarter in nine years. A real physical-AI edge-OS play, not a meme.
  • Figure ($FIGR) and Ouster ($OUST). Two fresh IPOs riding structural themes. $FIGR is Mike Cagney's blockchain-native lending marketplace, 55% EBITDA margins, HELOC origination costs of ~$800 versus an ~$10K industry average, ran from $40 to $78 on preliminary results. $OUST is a digital-lidar and perception platform for physical AI, Q1 revenue ~$49M up 49%, $175M cash and no debt, around $42 with a 12-month base case of $60 to $75. Both are swing-to-position candidates, not day trades.
  • The beaten-down mega-cap dip-buys. Invest with Henry is buying $SOFI under $18 (net income +134% year over year), $PLTR around $100 (half off its peak), $NVDA around $200 (target $300), and $MSFT at $373 (down 17% in a month). Fade the affiliate pitch, keep the names on the list.
  • Real-cash-flow crypto. With momentum bleeding, Milk Road is paying up for revenue: Galaxy Digital ($GLXY), whose "Helios" site is a 1.6 GW campus leased to CoreWeave for fifteen years at $1B-plus a year, and Sky ($SKY), the USDS stablecoin issuer that just did its best quarter ever at ~$124M revenue while the token fell 25%. The Milk Road Pro desk is adding $GLXY on the dip and rotating Micron profits into Marvell ($MRVL) as the next bottleneck link.
