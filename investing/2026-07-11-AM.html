The crowd spent the week getting maximally long while the rate market started pricing the opposite scenario, and this coming week carries the catalysts that settle it. Here is the week ahead, then six stories.

The week ahead. Monday is loaded. The Senate returns July 13 with roughly three working weeks to move the CLARITY Act before the August recess, SK Hynix converts from the when-issued $SKHYV line to regular-way $SKHY trading, and CBOE opens pre-market options trading on the megacaps ($TSLA, $NVDA, $META, $GOOGL, $AMD, $AAPL, $AMZN, $MSFT, $PLTR, $INTC), per the Stocks list.

Tuesday is June CPI, the week's binary, with Capital Flows expecting a disinflationary print. $NFLX reports Thursday July 16 as the first big-tech print, Josh's levels are a $76 reclaim to get long against a range down to $65. The July 29 Fed meeting is priced 66 percent hold, 34 percent hike.

On the tape itself, $SPY closed pressing 755 where dealers sit on $5.9 billion of positive gamma (hedging flow that pins price), with 752 as the line that cancels the up move. And Iran's deadline to publicly declare the Strait of Hormuz open lands today, with Kalshi pricing 38 percent odds oil trades above $74.99 by end of July.

1. Everyone got long at the same time, and the bond market is the only one hedging. The positioning stack, per Kalshi and the Stocks list: options traders at their most bullish since December 2020, hedge funds bought tech at the fastest pace since October, dollar positioning the most bullish since 2015, $600 billion of market cap added in three hours Thursday, and Kalshi gives 69 percent odds the S&P touches 8,000 this year. Barchart adds the valuation kicker, the Shiller PE (price against ten years of averaged earnings) is on the verge of taking out its dot-com record. Mr_Derivatives notes the VIX is the lowest since January 12 and is already positioning for an August vol event.

Now the other side of the ledger. Capital Flows points out the July 29 meeting is priced 66/34 hold versus hike with about 51 basis points of hikes priced to the terminal rate, the first time in years the market genuinely cannot decide if the next move is up. His plumbing detail: the December 2026 SOFR contract (futures on the overnight funding rate, the market's Fed-path bet) is now the largest by open interest in SOFR history, up 28 percent in about two months, and the 2026/2027 calendar spreads price just 6 basis points of cuts. If the Fed overtightens into disinflation, those spreads reprice violently, and that is his asymmetric trade.

His base case is not bearish, and the data backs him: nominal GDP running 5.8 percent, ISM manufacturing accelerating five to six prints in a row, industrials and financials at all-time highs. The point is narrower. The crowd is long at dot-com valuations while Kalshi prices a 50 percent chance the Fed's next move this year is a hike, and Berkshire is sitting on a record $397 billion of cash. Somebody in that picture is mispositioned, and Tuesday's CPI is the referee.

2. The memory trade gets its second act Monday, and the chart underneath it just went on probation. Follow-through from the debut, per the Market list: the SK Hynix chair says doubling 2025 capacity within five years still will not meet demand, his words being that memory is not a cyclical business anymore. Regular-way $SKHY trading starts Monday, Mr_Derivatives bought the when-issued line targeting $195 to $200, and the $SKHX 2x long and $SKHZ short ETFs launch Tuesday, per the People list. The supply concentration is the thesis in one stat: Samsung at 39.3 percent, Hynix at 28.9, and Micron at 22.8 hold about 90 percent of DRAM through 2027. Micron itself committed $250 billion to US memory build-out, per the Financial Markets list.

The chart is less cooperative than the story. Josh's read on $MU: the bounce off his $86 call ran to his $99.60 target, but the week failed to close above it, so he flips bearish into Monday, watching an air pocket toward $90 and $86.10 with $95 as his stop on longs. A reclaim of $99.60 early in the week re-arms the move toward $108.90 and his $125 breakout target.

Around the complex, $SNDK has been the best mover on the memory-supercycle theme with Monday decisive per ripster and Wujastyk, and Rosenblatt reports the optical shorts in $AAOI and $LITE plan to cover in late July and flip long for the 2027 co-packaged-optics cycle, per the Market list. One framing to keep from Capital Flows on X: $MU is trading on fundamental flows while $NVDA trades as market beta, so the memory names are where the actual AI-demand signal lives.

3. Bitcoin is still parked at $64,000, but the seller has a new name: the miners. The fork is unchanged from yesterday, per the Crypto list, low $70,000s if $64,000 keeps holding, a flush into the high $50,000s if it gives way, and Gillen himself leans toward the lower path while sitting in cash. What is new is the supply story. Saylor says miners are rotating their fleets into AI compute and selling their Bitcoin to fund it, and declares the four-year halving cycle broken.

Lyn Alden puts numbers on the decoupling: Bitcoin tracks global M2 (worldwide money supply) about 83 percent of the time, and this is the 17 percent, with treasury-company absorption gone and the AI chip trade draining the marginal liquidity. The relative charts agree, Bitcoin sits at a 29-month low against the S&P and gold at an 11-month low against it, per Mr_Derivatives.

The counter-current is institutional. Matt Hougan describes retail at peak despair while he logged the most investor meetings in a single day this year, and Bitwise colleague Ryan Watkins calls this the most excited he has been since 2023. On the policy calendar, SEC Commissioner Hester Peirce still expects the CLARITY Act (the SEC-versus-CFTC jurisdiction bill) to pass this summer while Polymarket prices 39 percent, and one of them is simply wrong, per the People list.

The overnight lists add the machinery: CFTC chair Selig calls the bill must-pass, French Hill holds a New York field hearing this week, and Senate Democrats led by Warren want hearings on Trump's crypto profits instead. Watch one liquidity flag under all of it, the BoJ balance sheet is down $726 billion from peak, and yen carry unwinds have a habit of showing up in crypto first.

For the institutions, Blueprint Finance CEO Nick Roberts-Huntley (ex-Point72) told Milk Road that BitGo is committing custodied assets into his Concrete vaults without leaving qualified custody, quoting over $1 billion by year-end, and he sees $ETH at new all-time highs within a couple of years. ETH staking just hit an all-time-high 33.5 percent of supply.

5. Meta added $230 billion in two days, and the legal demand sitting across from it is $1.4 trillion. The bull print, per the People list: about $230 billion of market cap added in under 48 hours after Muse Spark 1.1 shipped, from a superintelligence team that cost roughly $15 billion to assemble. MilkRoadMacro's valuation frame is that $META carries a PEG of 0.87 (price-to-earnings against growth, under 1 is cheap for the growth) versus Microsoft at 1.34 and Google at 1.47, and CheddarFlow flagged unusual million-dollar far-out-of-the-money call buying in $META and $MSFT into the weekend. The SemiAnalysis argument driving it: Meta is the only hyperscaler on track to be world-class at data, talent, and compute at once, with 3,000 engineers building nothing but reinforcement-learning environments.

Hold that against the Prof G segment on the same company: the four-state penalty demand runs to $1.4 trillion, a number that would consume over 90 percent of the company's value, with nearly 30 parallel state cases in federal court, $375 million already lost in New Mexico, and child-access bans spreading across 40-plus countries. The guest's frame is that Meta is in its big-tobacco moment, with whistleblower documents showing it knew the harm and kept going. Both stories are live at once. The market is paying up for the AI lab and assigning the litigation a zero, and litigation zeros have a way of becoming numbers.

6. XRP just printed a reading it has shown once in twelve years, and the confirmation level is spelled out. Lark Davis walks the data: the weekly RSI (momentum oscillator) hit oversold for only the second time in $XRP's history, the second-lowest reading ever, and the first occurrence preceded one of its most violent rallies roughly two years later. MVRV (aggregate holder profit and loss against cost basis) sits at a record negative 45 to 47 percent, the shape of prior capitulation bottoms. Flows are diverging from the majors, $1.49 billion of cumulative inflows into seven US spot XRP ETFs and 970 million XRP locked while Bitcoin ETFs bled hundreds of millions daily, with active addresses up 72 percent in two weeks.

His gate is unambiguous, a weekly close back above the 200-week moving average, the long-run trend line XRP has spent three of the last ten years below. Until that prints, the data is a setup and nothing else. The fundamental hook is Garlinghouse claiming Ripple touches $16 trillion in annual payments and clearing with digital assets at essentially zero percent of it, plus the proposed XLS-65 and XLS-66 lending protocols for borrowing against tokenized treasuries on-chain.

Psychology. One trade from the overnight tape says everything about where the crowd's head is. Per lookonchain, a trader who lost $4.4 million on the Hynix run turned around and re-longed $30.17 million of the same exposure at 2x leverage, and a second account opened a $20 million 3x long on $NVDA perps into the weekend. The crypto room is the mirror image. Hougan's peak-despair-plus-packed-calendar observation and Watkins locking in his bullishness describe a market where the retail crowd left exactly as the suits arrived, and Saylor's miners are selling the asset to buy AI compute at the top of the other crowd's euphoria.

So the check this weekend is simple. If you are tempted to pre-load Monday longs so you do not miss the 755 break, remember you would be buying the most crowded positioning in six years two days before a CPI print, with the Fed openly discussing hikes. If you are sitting on a loss from this week, the Hynix revenge trade is your cautionary portrait, size the re-entry like the first loss taught you something. The levels are on the page, $64,000, $99.60, 752. Let the tape come to them.
