The crowd got more stretched today while the smart money quietly sold its winners and bought power and neoclouds

Global equity funds took in $56.4 billion, the Shiller PE is on the verge of its dot-com record, and Berkshire is sitting on $397 billion of cash, yet the rotation underneath sold Robinhood and Hyperliquid into strength and bought Nebius, CoreWeave, and Vistra. SK Hynix's ADR ran 13 percent on day one, Robinhood Chain flipped Base, and Ethereum's ratio against Bitcoin broke an eleven-month downtrend.

Market's closed, so this is about positioning, and the split widened today. The visible crowd kept piling into the same longs while the money that actually moves rotated out of this week's winners and into the power-and-compute plumbing. Here is the week ahead, then five stories.

The week ahead. Monday the Senate returns and SK Hynix converts to regular-way $SKHY, with the $SKHX 2x long and $SKHZ short ETFs launching Tuesday, per the Financial Markets list. Tuesday is June CPI, still the week's binary. $NFLX reports Thursday July 16 as the first big-tech print, with options implying a 8.3 percent move after the stock fell in 11 of the last 13 weeks. The July 29 Fed meeting sits 66 percent priced to hold, and the CLARITY Act (the crypto market-structure bill splitting SEC and CFTC oversight) gets roughly three Senate working weeks before the August recess, with Polymarket at 40 percent it passes, per the Crypto list.

1. The real tell today was the rotation: sell the crypto and fintech winners, buy the neoclouds and the power names. Milk Road's PRO desk ran 16 trades this week and the direction is clean. They sold $HOOD (Robinhood) at $112.79 for a 55 percent gain and $HYPE (Hyperliquid) around $71 for 135 percent, then bought $NBIS (Nebius) at $199 and $CRWV (CoreWeave) at $81 to $82. That is profit-taking on the retail-flow names and re-entry into the companies that rent out GPUs.

The demand data underneath the neocloud bet is real. The same desk logged Google Cloud crossing $20 billion in a single quarter, up 63 percent year over year with backlog nearly doubling to $462 billion, and AWS growing 28 percent on a $150 billion run rate with Bedrock now past 125,000 enterprise customers. If those backlogs are real orders, the picks-and-shovels layer gets paid before the application layer does.

The power trade is the other half of the same idea. A Reddit DD lays out $VST (Vistra) at a forward PE near 15 and a PEG (price-to-earnings against growth, under 1 is cheap) around 0.5, with its Cogentrix acquisition and power deals signed with Amazon, Meta, and a Kuwaiti sovereign fund. Tom Nash's five-stock list lands on the same seam from the hardware side, naming $VRT (Vertiv, data-center cooling) with revenue up 27 percent and operating income up to $2 billion, a ten-fold gain in three years. Data centers need power and cooling before they need another model, and the market is finally pricing that in order.

2. Robinhood Chain did not just take half of Solana, it flipped Base. The update from the Crypto list: Robinhood Chain now does more daily DEX volume (decentralized-exchange trading) than Base, Coinbase's own chain. Deposited assets crossed $300 million, up about 300 percent week over week, and roughly $100 million of Ether has been bridged in, a ten-fold jump, because the chain uses $ETH as its gas token. That last detail is the one to hold, a successful Robinhood Chain is now a live source of new Ether demand.

The adoption numbers are early but loud. The chain has 194,000 daily active addresses and 100-millisecond block times against Solana's 400, and its 37,000 tokenized-equity holders already make up 6.9 percent of everyone holding tokenized stock anywhere, a week and a half after launch. The August test from the AM edition still stands, hold the volume or it was a launch-week spike, but the trend since this morning went straight up.

3. The memory supercycle keeps compounding, and Monday it gets its own trillion-dollar ticker. SK Hynix's US-listed shares ran 13 percent on their first day on $18 billion of volume, the biggest ADR debut since Alibaba, and the name now sits in the trillion-dollar club, per the Financial Markets and People lists. BNP's framing is that this is still 1998 in the analogy, early in the cycle rather than the blow-off top.

Micron gave the numbers that justify the story. Per a detailed Reddit breakdown, $MU raised its planned US investment past $250 billion through 2035 and its Cloud Memory unit grew revenue from $3.4 billion to $13.8 billion in a year while operating margin went from 46 percent to 78 percent. It also committed $500 million to GlobalWafers for a Texas facility on a ten-year supply deal. The year-to-date scoreboard tells you where the crowd already is, $SNDK is up 707 percent, $MU 243 percent, and $WDC 238 percent, per the People list.

One caution worth keeping. The fab timing is years out, first Idaho wafers in mid-2027 and a second fab in late 2028, and the 2018 memory glut is the reason nobody should treat 78 percent margins as permanent.

The names that actually worked this year back the thesis anyway. A mid-year audit of 30 finance YouTubers found the winners clustered on Micron, AMD, and Marvell, with the top performer up 101 percent on Micron and Marvell and the runner-up up 83 percent on an AMD position that itself gained 165 percent. The losers sat in software and consumer names like Adobe, Salesforce, and Nike. The hardware-and-power trade is not a forecast, it is already the year's scoreboard.

4. The AI-capex bill finally got a real number, and the hyperscalers do not all pass. A sharp reverse-DCF post worked the discounted-cash-flow math backwards, asking what growth each hyperscaler's price already assumes. To justify $362, $GOOGL has to compound revenue at 25.7 percent a year, eight points above consensus, or lift margins from 38 to 52 percent, so the stock is priced to beat the Street. $MSFT screens the other way, a fair value near $483 against a $383 price, meaning the selloff built in a cushion. $META sits right on its line around $637 versus $603.

That squares with what moved Meta this week. Per the Market list, the re-rate has a named cause, Muse Spark 1.1 shipping at roughly a quarter of OpenAI and Anthropic pricing, and the stock reclaimed its 200-day average per the Kalshi list. Cheap inference is the whole bull case, and the market is paying for it while quietly pricing the $1.4 trillion in state penalty demands at zero.

5. Ethereum's ratio against Bitcoin broke its downtrend, and Bitcoin sits on the line that decides July. The fresh technical, per the Crypto list: ETH/BTC posted its first weekly close above an eleven-month downtrend, with whales accumulating 270,000 Bitcoin around $59,000 and a relative-strength target near $76,000. On Ether's own chart the level to hold is $1,820. The demand sink behind it is concrete, digital-asset treasury companies now hold about 7 percent of all Ether supply and throw off $400 million to $500 million a year in staking revenue they are bound to reinvest, per Milk Road.

Bitcoin itself is unchanged and coiled. Hold $64,000 and the path is $70,000 to $71,000, lose it and $58,000 opens, and John Gillen still leans toward the lower path while sitting in cash. Standard Chartered's Geoff Kendrick put a longer marker down, $100,000 by the end of 2026 and $200,000 by the end of 2027, per the Financial Markets list. Near-term the CLARITY vote is the catalyst and $64,000 is the referee.

  • $PLTR (Palantir). Tom Nash's top pick, 56 percent revenue growth, 360 percent operating-income growth, and a rule-of-40 score of 140, now down about 50 percent from its November 2025 high near $130. High multiple, real growth, cheaper than it was.
  • $NFLX (Netflix). Reports Thursday with options implying an 8.3 percent move, down 35 percent across 11 of the last 13 weeks, per the Financial Markets list. Oversold into the first big print of earnings season.

Per Milk Road, 59 percent revenue growth and 85 percent EBITDA margins, trading near $527 against a $660 fair-value estimate, but weighed by SEC scrutiny and roughly $195 million of insider selling in 90 days. The open question is whether its AXON ad engine works outside gaming.
  • $VPG / $AMBA (Vishay Precision, Ambarella). Lark Davis's robot picks, a tactile-sensor maker with book-to-bill at 1.21 and an edge-AI vision-chip name still priced as an auto supplier before any robotics re-rating.
  • $BOT / Agility Robotics (Robo Strategy fund, Agility). The same list flags the closed-end $BOT trading at a 3-times premium to its net asset value, a real risk, while Agility (the Digit humanoid in Amazon warehouses) comes public by SPAC around September as the first pure-play public humanoid.
  • $SPCX (SpaceX). Now the sixth-largest name in the Nasdaq 100 a month after its IPO at a $2.14 trillion cap, with Cathie Wood adding 116,971 shares, per the People list. It round-tripped below its open today, and Milk Road stopped out at $155.80 after Meta signaled it will build its own compute. The AI-pivot story is real (a $6.3 billion Reflection AI compute deal at $150 million a month) but the stock trades at 115 times sales.
  • $CRWD vs $S (CrowdStrike, SentinelOne). Per a head-to-head breakdown, CrowdStrike is the steadier compounder with roughly 6 percent to its analyst target while SentinelOne carries up to 48 percent upside as the higher-risk catch-up trade.

Psychology. The cleanest read today is the gap between who is buying and who is selling. The visible crowd added to the most crowded book in years, global funds took in $56.4 billion in a week and the Shiller PE (price against ten years of averaged earnings) neared its dot-com record, per the Kalshi list, while Berkshire sat on $397 billion of cash and Milk Road's desk sold its 55 percent and 135 percent winners into the strength. When the fast money is ringing the register on $HOOD and $HYPE and the slow money is holding a record cash pile, the tape is telling you the easy part of this move already happened.

Retail is doing the opposite, panic-bridging onto Robinhood Chain as it goes vertical and re-longing the same memory names that burned traders this week. The bias that setup breeds is chasing, buying the 194,000-address chart or the $SNDK-up-707-percent screen precisely because it already ran. The Hynix revenge trade from this morning is the portrait of where that ends.

So the weekend check is about your own hands. If you are tempted to buy something this weekend, ask whether you are buying it because the thesis is fresh or because the chart already paid someone else. The rotation into $VST and $NBIS is people selling what worked to buy what has not moved yet, and that is the harder, better trade than adding to the vertical. The levels are on the page, $64,000, $1,820, the CPI print Tuesday. Let them come to you.
