The ceasefire Trump floated at 1am was dead by afternoon, oil ripped back toward $80, and the tape still spent the day buying chips into SK Hynix's debut tomorrow

This morning the market bought the war on a deal tweet. By the close Trump said it is over and called Iran scum, crude reversed 6 to 7 percent higher, and rate-hike odds jumped to 56 percent, and none of it stopped the biggest one-day semiconductor rotation in months. Here is the close, then the six things that matter into Friday.

StocksMacroBitcoinCrypto

The morning story flipped on itself. At 1am Trump said Iran wanted a deal and the selloff went green. By afternoon he said the ceasefire was over, the US launched another round of strikes, and oil that had been falling on the deal hope ripped 6 to 7 percent back toward $80. And yet the tape did something strange. It kept buying semiconductors all day, straight into SK Hynix listing tomorrow. Here is the close, then the six things that matter.

The close. Green where it counts, and the count is chips. Per the Financial Markets list, crude that sold off on the morning deal talk reversed hard once Trump killed the ceasefire, with WTI back near $74 and Brent near $78, and Mohamed El-Erian flagged the cross-asset tell that oil is now dragging global bond yields around more than the Fed is. Per the Kalshi list, the odds of a rate hike before year end climbed to 56 percent and the NY Fed survey has one-year inflation expectations up another 3.7 percent, the hottest in years. The dovish Fed minutes from this morning already look dated. Under all of it, the money rotated into the one trade that does not care about Tehran.

1. SK Hynix lists tomorrow, the deal got upsized, and Samsung's drop is the warning. The lead moved overnight. Per the People list, the SK Hynix ADR priced at $149, about 3 percent above the Korea close and more than 7 times oversubscribed, and the raise was lifted to roughly $28 to $29 billion, which if it holds is the largest ADR listing on record, topping Alibaba's $21.8 billion in 2014. The leveraged ladder is set, with 2x-long $SKHX and 1x-short $SKHZ following Monday. Per the Stocks list, Jim Cramer's warning is to price it in the hole or watch hedge funds fade it.

Here is the counter nobody wants next to the hype. Per the People list, Samsung guided to a record quarter, 89.4 trillion won in sales and 17.1 trillion in operating profit, and the stock fell anyway on softer AI-memory expectations. A record print that gets sold is exactly the read-through you carry into tomorrow's debut.

2. Micron committed $250 billion and the whole semi complex ran with it. The rotation was real and it had a leaderboard. Per the Stocks list, Micron raised its US investment plan to more than $250 billion through 2035 with the first pour at its Clay, New York fab, targeting roughly 40 percent of its DRAM made domestically, and $MU jumped about 6.5 percent back over $1,000 a share with shorts including Michael Burry reportedly scrambling to cover. Per the Market list and the People list, the one-day tape ran deep, $AEHR up 14 percent, $LITE up 13, $KLAC up 12, $ARM up 11, $AMAT up 10, $AMKR up 10, $MRVL and $MU up 7, $AMD up 6, $ASML and $INTC up 5. BofA's read is that AI spending is shifting from the hyperscalers to the chipmakers, and today the tape agreed.

3. Meta went from too much compute to not enough, and the neoclouds ripped. This is the reversal that reframes the week. Per the Stocks list, a week ago the fear was Meta had excess compute, the story that sank the neocloud names. Today the report is Meta plans to double compute to 14 gigawatts by 2027 and starts building its in-house Iris chip, designed with $AVGO and made at $TSM, in September. Per the Market list, Wolfe now models Meta guiding to roughly $220 billion of 2027 capex against a $160 billion street number, and the read-through lifted $NBIS, $IREN, and $WULF. The level to watch is $META holding $600 after it round-tripped from a $577 morning dip back to $606.

4. Nvidia and AMD both sit on lines into Friday. The two biggest names have clean levels. Per the Stocks list, $NVDA has to hold above 198.6 into Friday or the setup reverses down hard, with the upside retest at the 50-day near 209, tighter than this morning's 200-day-near-$190 frame. Per the People list, $AMD is trying to break 542 to 551 toward a new high, now carrying a bigger market cap than JPMorgan and Walmart and about 90 percent of the way to a trillion dollars. Hold the lines and the rotation has room. Lose them and the whole complex gives it back.

5. Oil coming back is the quiet risk to everything else. Respect the crude tape. Per the Financial Markets list, the moment the ceasefire died, oil reversed its morning drop and pushed 6 to 7 percent higher, Qatar halted its LNG ramp after the Hormuz strikes, and Goldman's note is to watch the oil path over the crude spot number because escalation or negotiation decides the next leg. Sustained crude above $75 puts the inflation channel back in play right as the Fed's hike odds are climbing. The bulls got their chip rotation. The bears got their oil.

6. Bitcoin's bottom signals keep flashing while Saylor becomes a seller. The structure firmed even as the biggest holder flipped. Per the Crypto list, Bitcoin sits near $62,750, up about 2.6 percent on the week, and Scott Melker says almost every indicator seen at prior bottoms is hitting now, the 200-week average, oversold RSI on the high timeframes. Lyn Alden sharpened her call, that capital rotates back to crypto only once the fast money finishes selling, and her trigger is the AI names topping out. Per 0xResearch, Bitcoin took the Iran shock like a tech stock, down 2.5 percent at worst then steady, while $350 million of altcoin longs got liquidated Wednesday.

The seller is the story though. Per the Crypto list, Michael Saylor sold Bitcoin for the first time, about $216 million, to fund his STRC dividend, and blames the coin's lag on miners rotating into AI compute and selling their coins to pay for it. Barclays still initiated $MSTR a buy at $130 and now calls it a fintech, not a crypto proxy. And in a real shift, Robinhood put a perpetuals exchange in front of its 28 million customers and picked Lighter, not Hyperliquid, which knocked $HYPE.

Single names worth your watchlist.

  • $VRT (Vertiv). Per Rick Orford, the cleanest AI-power and cooling name for the Nvidia GB200 racks, first-quarter sales up 30 percent to about $2.65 billion with margins up 430 basis points, and it raised full-year guidance to $13.5 to $14 billion. It trades near 48 times forward earnings, so the growth has to keep showing up.
  • $IREN. Per Rick Orford, the Bitcoin miner turning its renewable campuses into AI cloud, now sitting on a $9.7 billion Microsoft deal and a $3.4 billion Nvidia deal with Dell supplying Blackwell racks. The re-rate is whether the market prices it as AI infrastructure instead of a cyclical miner.
  • $APP (AppLovin). Per Milk Road, the ad marketplace trades near $527 against a probability-weighted fair value around $660, with revenue up 59 percent and an 85 percent EBITDA margin, and the stated plan is to start buying under $500 and go full size near $400 to $430. The whole bet is whether the AXON engine generalizes past gaming, and the CEO just sold $51 million of stock.
  • $NBIS (Nebius). Per the Stocks list, the neocloud with a $49 billion backlog, $27 billion from Meta and up to $19.4 billion from Microsoft, first-quarter revenue up 684 percent, carrying a $300 year-end target. The purest lever on the Meta compute reversal above.
  • $VECO (Veeco). Per one Reddit teardown, the picks-and-shovels way to own the memory buildout without the memory-price risk, the tool-of-record maker for the laser annealing that HBM stacks need, at a roughly $3.5 billion cap with an Axcelis merger waiting only on China approval.
  • $SOFI. Per Invest with Henry, the fintech near $18.32 with members up 35 percent and 2026 guidance around $4.65 billion, reports July 29, and the stated buy zone is under $20.

Psychology. Watch what the crowd did with the same headline twice in one day. This morning it bought the war because Trump hinted at a deal. This afternoon he said the deal was dead, called Iran scum, sent oil ripping, and the market bought again, this time into a chip rotation and an IPO priced tomorrow. That is a tape that has decided the direction in advance and is reading every headline, good or bad, as a reason to stay long the thing it already wanted to own. The danger in that is not the war. It is that conviction this smooth stops checking itself. When both the ceasefire and the collapse of the ceasefire are bullish, you are no longer reacting to news, you are just rationalizing a position.

Now do the discipline part on yourself. The honest signal today was not the green screen, it was Samsung. Per the People list, it printed a record quarter and the stock fell, because the good news was already owned. Hold that next to your own SK Hynix or Nvidia position going into Friday and ask the uncomfortable version of the question. If the thing you are long printed a perfect number and still dropped, what were you actually being paid for. Per the Crypto list, Michael Saylor sold his coins for the first time and told everyone why, and the crowd's response was to call it a buy signal. Sometimes the person selling is not sending a message. Sometimes he just needs the cash, and the story you build around his sale is the one you will be holding when the level breaks.
