The whole argument about whether AI spending is about to collapse gets settled on Wednesday when Alphabet reports, and everything trading this week is really a bet on that one number

Alphabet's capital-spending guide is the referee for a memory complex down 20 percent and a market 2 percent from its record. Korea's stock crisis got worse overnight, oil pushed back over $90, and SpaceX just got a $900 price target the same week its share count starts to explode.

MacroStocksCrypto

A Monday that is really a countdown to Wednesday. The market spent last week deciding the AI trade might be broken, and the company most able to prove or disprove that reports in 48 hours. Here is the pre-open picture, then five stories.

Pre-open preview. Futures are flattish into a heavy week. The S&P sits about 2 percent below its early-June record while the Philadelphia semiconductor index is more than 20 percent off its late-June high yet still up 60 percent on the year, per the Market list. More than 80 S&P companies report this week with Alphabet on Wednesday the clear catalyst, Tesla and Intel later in the stretch, and the Fed meeting at month-end. Overnight the trouble was abroad, with Korea's market down another 4 percent, oil back over $90, and gold struggling near $4,000 after its worst week in six, per the Financial Markets list. SpaceX flies its rescheduled Starship test Thursday, not today.

1. Alphabet on Wednesday is the referee for the entire AI-capex fear. The single print that matters is a capital-spending number. Per the Market list, Alphabet reports Wednesday as the first hyperscaler read of the season, and a pullback in its AI capex guidance would ripple straight through the memory and neocloud names that just sold off, while a confident guide would tell the market the buildout is intact.

The fundamentals give the bulls a strong hand going in. Per the Stocks list, Alphabet trades near 22 times forward earnings as the cheapest of the Magnificent Seven, its cloud revenue grew 63 percent to $20 billion with the backlog doubled to $462 billion, and its own chips now sell to Anthropic and Meta. The whole week is positioned around this one report, so the risk is not the number itself but how crowded the bet on it has become.

2. The memory bull case has a cleaner name than Micron, and it is SK Hynix. The trade beneath the AI trade is high-bandwidth memory, and the leader is Korean. Per the People list, Nvidia cannot walk away from SK Hynix because it supplies 70 percent of the memory going into the Vera Rubin platform on co-designed chips, holding 56 percent of the market to Micron's 23, and on Shay Boloor's growth-adjusted screen SK Hynix is the cheapest name in the group at a fraction of Micron's multiple.

The structural case underneath is a cash-flow inversion. Per the Crypto list, Gavin Baker notes that Nvidia, Micron, Broadcom, and Applied Materials are on pace for roughly $430 billion of combined free cash flow over the next year, more than triple two years ago, even as the five hyperscalers' free cash flow falls from around $250 billion in 2024 toward $100 billion by the end of 2026 under an estimated $1.8 trillion of AI spending. The money is visibly moving from the spenders to the suppliers, and Micron itself sits down 30 percent with its memory sold out through 2027, per the Market list.

3. SpaceX got a $900 target the same week its float starts to flood. The most-owned new stock got a bullish number with a catch. Per Lark Davis, Citi set a $900 price target on SpaceX, but the timing matters more than the target, because the public float jumps from about 5 percent now to 60 percent by November as pre-IPO lockups expire, and toward 100 percent by mid-2027.

That is a tsunami of supply regardless of fair value. His framing is the right one, that you do not want to be the exit liquidity for the venture investors who are about to be freed to sell, so the discipline is to let the lockup selling exhaust itself before reaching for Citi's target. The stock is already down about 45 percent from its highs, per the Market list, and the wall of new shares is the reason a great company can still be a bad stock for a few more months.

4. Oil is back over $90 and it is the quiet threat to the whole tape. The move that could break the calm is in crude. Per the People list, Brent jumped back over $90 a barrel as the Iran war entered its tenth day with the Strait of Hormuz still blocked, and gold sold off hard into the strength, posting its biggest weekly drop in six.

The read-through is that the disinflation trade the Fed leaned on is getting more expensive by the week. One rotation call making the rounds is to be long oil from the seventies, move out of semis and software into cyclical financials and healthcare, and stay defensive through the illiquid summer, per the Crypto list. Whether or not you make that exact trade, a crude benchmark over $90 into a Fed meeting is the variable that can override even a good Alphabet print.

5. Crypto is holding its levels while the structural bears sharpen their case. The majors are steady into the week. Per the Crypto list, Bitcoin is back over $65,000 and Ether is pinned to the $2,000 pivot that decides its next move, a close above pointing higher and a loss of it opening a sweep of the lows.

The bearish argument getting louder is Chamath Palihapitiya's. Per the People list, he lays out two structural headwinds for Bitcoin, that speculative liquidity now prefers prediction markets and equities, and that the energy going into Bitcoin mining is worth ten to twenty times more if it is reallocated to running AI models. That is a real long-term question about whether the marginal buyer and the marginal watt both have somewhere better to go, and it is the kind of thesis that matters more the longer the price stays stuck.

Single names to track.

  • $GOOGL (Alphabet). Reports Wednesday as the referee for the AI-capex debate, cheapest Magnificent Seven name near 22 times forward with cloud up 63 percent and a $462 billion backlog, per the Stocks list.
  • SK Hynix. The cleanest memory name, supplying 70 percent of Vera Rubin's high-bandwidth memory at 56 percent market share and the lowest growth-adjusted multiple in the group, per the People list.
  • $MU (Micron). Down 30 percent with memory sold out through 2027, bouncing off its falling-wedge lows on Friday with levels stacked from 806 to 906, per the Market list and Stocks list.
  • $SPCX (SpaceX). Carries a fresh Citi $900 target but faces a float going from 5 to 60 percent by November, down 45 percent from its highs, per Lark Davis.
  • BTC (Bitcoin). Back over $65,000 against Chamath's two structural headwinds of liquidity and energy competition, per the Crypto list.

Psychology. Look east before you look at your own screen. Korea is running the same AI-chip mania the US is, and its market volatility just hit the highest level in its history, worse than the financial crisis, with the index down 30 percent from a record it set only last month, per the Financial Markets list. That is what a crowded, levered version of this exact trade looks like when it unwinds, and it is happening in real time to people running the same thesis you are.

The tell of the moment is how eager the crowd is to call the bottom anyway. The dip-buyers are naming Micron's floor to the dollar and Bitcoin's low to the level, the promoters are quoting Citi's $900 on SpaceX while ignoring the 60 percent float unlock behind it, and everyone is treating Friday's bounce as the all-clear into a week that hinges on a single earnings print. Confidence is cheap the weekend after a scare, and it is usually most abundant right before the scare's second leg.

So the discipline into Alphabet on Wednesday is to hold your size and your patience. The memory demand is real and sold out through 2027, but the cleanest way to own it is the supplier with the lowest multiple, not the leveraged expression that round-trips on a headline. Let Alphabet print before you decide the AI trade is fixed, respect that Korea is showing you the downside of crowding, and keep enough cash that oil over $90 or a soft capex guide is your opportunity rather than your problem. The market wants you certain this morning. The move is to stay liquid and let Wednesday do the talking.
