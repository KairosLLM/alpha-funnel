The tape was green every hour of the session, and underneath it the smartest money and the loudest bear were both quietly selling the leaders

Stocks ran a clean trend day up on the first session back, but Aschenbrenner's fund is short the chips and long the power, Michael Burry says the end is near, and the money is rotating from the design layer into the stuff that keeps the lights on. AMD led, Micron faded, and Saylor sold Bitcoin into a shrug.

StocksMacroBitcoinCrypto

Two tapes again, and today they sat right on top of each other. On the surface it was a picture-perfect melt-up, orderly and green into the close. One layer down, the people with the best track records spent the day rotating out of the exact names that led. Here is the recap, then the rotation nobody on the ticker was talking about.

The close. The first session back from the long weekend was a clean trend day up. Per the Financial Markets list, Linda Raschke called it a pure trend day out of a three-bar triangle with no reversal, the S&P held near 7520 into the bell, and the running joke was that the market has been green every day of July. Per the People-to-follow list, $AMD was the standout up more than 10% as shorts got liquidated and Lisa Su appeared at a Trump event, while ISM Services printed 54 with a firm 51.2 employment read, and the Fear and Greed index sits at a neutral 45.

$DELL ran 8% after Trump told people to go buy one and Michael Dell pledged billions to child accounts. SpaceX joins the Nasdaq 100 tonight under $SPCX, forcing more than $4B of passive buying at the close. The one date that matters this week is still the FOMC minutes on Wednesday July 8.

1. The smartest money spent the day rotating out of the chips and into the power. This is the story under the melt-up. Per Invest with Henry, Leopold Aschenbrenner's roughly $20B fund is running about $8.5B of downside against the crowded design layer, with put exposure to the $SMH semi ETF, $NVDA, $AVGO, $AMD, $MU, $TSM, and $ASML (the sole supplier of the EUV lithography every advanced node needs, per Rick Orford), while going long the deployment layer instead: Bloom Energy $BE, SanDisk $SNDK, CoreWeave $CRWV, and $IREN. It is not an anti-AI bet, it is a bet that the market overpays the chip designers and underpays the power, memory, and data-center slots that actually bind.

The institutional read agrees. Per Adam Parker, the former Morgan Stanley strategist, we are in the seventh inning on stock prices but only the third on fundamentals, and the tell he is watching is correlation: Eaton, GE Vernova, Vistra, Constellation, and even gold now track the AI-semis basket more tightly than Nvidia does. His advice is to pair semis with energy and healthcare, the two things that do not move as one when the AI trade wobbles.

2. Michael Burry stopped hedging his words and said the end is near. The loudest bear got louder. Per the Polymarket list and the Kalshi list, Michael Burry followed yesterday's $MU short by declaring the end is near for AI stocks and an imminent major collapse, and Morgan Stanley is now openly describing the AI trade as rotating away from the leaders with semis momentum fading. You do not have to trade on Burry, whose timing is famously early, but when the man who called the last blowup and the biggest sell-side desk are pointing at the same rotation the tape just ran, it is worth knowing which side of it you are on.

3. Memory gapped up at the open and then faded all day, and the calendar is why. The morning's melt-up did not hold in the one name it was built on. Per the Stocks list, $MU gapped up more than 100 points on the Sunday-night open and then lagged the rest of semis for the entire session, unable to reclaim its near-term moving average even as $AMD ripped. The overhang has a date on it. Per the People-to-follow list, SK Hynix filed to list in the US under $SKHY by the end of this week, raising around $28B in the largest US IPO marketing in memory, and Aschenbrenner's own fund is a cornerstone buyer, so the memory bid is rotating into the fresh paper. The offset is real demand: Micron signed a strategic supply agreement to put its memory in Ford's next-generation vehicles, and $MU is now the third-largest weight in the Nasdaq 100.

4. The money that left the chips showed up in power and the neoclouds. If you want to see where the rotation landed, follow the electricity. Per a Goldman map relayed by Milk Road, of the $7.6T headed into AI infrastructure through 2031, power is a small slice but the single gate that can stall everything downstream, which puts scarce-asset owners in the path of the capital. Per Rick Orford, GE Vernova $GEV is pricing new gas turbines 10 to 20% above last quarter into a book that is sold out to 2030, throwing off $4.8B of free cash in a single quarter against a $163B backlog.

And per Barchart and Yahoo Finance, Oklo $OKLO just won DOE safety approval for its test reactor and is building a 1.2 gigawatt Ohio campus to power Meta data centers, even after the stock fell 22% in June when the DOE floated a $17.5B loan program aimed at big traditional reactors instead of small modular ones.

The neoclouds, the high-beta version of the same trade, ripped today. Per the People-to-follow list, $IREN jumped 11% on a reported Anthropic data-center deal in Australia, $CRWV added 5%, and $WULF is carrying a roughly $19B twenty-year Anthropic lease. Own the picks and shovels if you want the theme with less blow-up risk than the levered cloud rentiers.

5. Broadcom is quietly building the chip that pressures Nvidia's whole story. One more crack in the design-layer thesis. Per Rick Orford, Broadcom $AVGO co-designed OpenAI's first custom inference chip, taped out in nine months and targeting gigawatt-scale deployments starting this year. That is a direct shot at Nvidia's pricing power on general-purpose GPUs. The catch is valuation. $AVGO trades north of 53 times earnings against Nvidia near 23, so the custom-silicon future is already partly in the price, and its revenue leans on a handful of giant buyers.

6. Saylor sold Bitcoin for the first time in four years, and the market shrugged it off. The overhang everyone feared arrived, and the tape absorbed it. Per Milk Road, Michael Saylor sold 3,588 Bitcoin for about $216M at an average near $60,197 to fund his preferred dividends, still holds around 850,000 coins, and has flagged room to sell up to $800M more, a known seller sitting above any rally. Yet per the Crypto list, the reaction was positive, with $MSTR and the $STRC preferred both firming and Bitcoin holding $61,000 to $62,000 into the close.

The bottom-callers are getting louder underneath the fear. Per Lark Davis, spot Bitcoin ETFs just flipped back to a $200M inflow, Bernstein notes the 54% drawdown from the $125K peak is far milder than the 75 to 90% declines of past cycles, and altcoin sell pressure has fallen to a multi-year low that usually marks capitulation. Per Jordi Visser, the two forces that crushed crypto, a Fed repricing from cuts to hikes and capital fleeing into AI infrastructure, are both close to exhaustion, and the level to watch is clean: $64,000 reclaimed turns the tape, $60,000 lost brings the $55,000 bid into play. Per 0xResearch, Bitcoin actually closed the day slightly ahead of both the Nasdaq and gold, and the money inside crypto is rotating toward the things that print cash, the exchange tokens and the prediction-market venues, rather than the high-beta majors.

Single names worth your watchlist.

  • $GEV (GE Vernova). Per Rick Orford, turbines sold out to 2030 with 10 to 20% pricing power and a backlog heading from $163B toward $200B. The risk is conversion, since a chunk of the pipeline is slot reservations on data centers that still need permits, fuel, and financing. A buy-on-dips name, not a chase after a double.
  • $OKLO (Oklo). Per Barchart, DOE safety approval in hand and a 1.2 gigawatt Meta campus in the plan, with a consensus target near $84 against a low-$50s price. Pre-revenue and policy-whipsawed, so size it as a call option on nuclear, not a core hold.
  • $CCXI (Agility Robotics). Per Milk Road, the first pure-play humanoid stock, a SPAC merging with Agility at a $2.5B valuation, whose Digit robot has logged 65,000 operating hours and $300M in binding orders with Amazon, Toyota, and Schaeffler backing it. It burned $100M in cash last year, so this is a multi-year venture bet in a public wrapper.
  • $PFE (Pfizer). Per Stocks with Josh, the same $23.50 entry from this morning, trading below its pre-COVID price at a 6 to 7% yield with the revenue-cliff fear priced in. Accumulate in a range, do not lump in.
  • $QBTS (D-Wave). Per Rick Orford, fifteen analysts rate it a strong buy with targets implying a possible double, helped by $100M of proposed government funding, though the valuation rests entirely on quantum becoming a real layer rather than on today's financials.

Psychology. Notice how good today felt. Green every hour, $AMD up double digits with the shorts carried out, SpaceX forcing four billion dollars of buying into the bell, the Fear and Greed needle sitting at a comfortable neutral. Everything about the tape invited you to relax and add. That is precisely the moment to look at who was selling into it. Per Invest with Henry, the sharpest young manager in the game is short the chips you want to chase, and per the Polymarket list, Michael Burry is telling anyone who will listen that the end is near. A green trend day does not make them right, but it is the exact backdrop where you are most tempted to oversize into the names they are fading.

Then look at Bitcoin for the mirror image. Saylor sold for the first time in four years, the single most bearish headline a Bitcoin holder could imagine, and per the Crypto list the market simply shrugged and held $61,000. When the worst-case news lands and the price refuses to break, that tells you something the fear does not. The discipline tonight is symmetrical. Do not let a flawless up day talk you into full size at the top of a rotation, and do not let a scary headline talk you out of a position that is quietly refusing to fall. The tape rewards the person who feels both pulls and sizes off the plan anyway.
