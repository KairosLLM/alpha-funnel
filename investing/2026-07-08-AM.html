The US bombed Iran overnight and oil jumped 5%, so the complacency that ran the tape for a week gets its first real test at the open

A strike on 80-plus targets after Iran hit ships in Hormuz put a war premium back in crude just as the FOMC minutes land, and underneath it three separate analysts say Google, Oracle, and SpaceX all fail a cash-flow test. Nvidia's 200-day is the line that decides the rest. Here is the open.

StocksMacroBitcoinCrypto

Two things happened while you slept. The US struck Iran and oil spiked, and the calm that let the market shrug off a memory crash for a week suddenly has a reason to crack. Under the geopolitics, the real story is quieter and more dangerous: the bill for the AI buildout is coming due, and the DCF models are starting to name names. Here is the open, then the six things that matter.

The open. Risk-off, and geopolitics is driving. Per the Market list, the US struck more than 80 targets in Iran overnight after Iran attacked three commercial vessels in the Strait of Hormuz, WTI is up about 5%, and the S&P closed down 0.5% with the $SMH semis off 4% into yesterday's bell. Per the Financial Markets list, the Treasury revoked Iran's oil-export license and LNG transit through Hormuz has halted, with one trader calling for oil to see $80 before $60.

The FOMC minutes land this afternoon into a genuine paradox. Per the Financial Markets list, futures now price the Fed hiking in 2026 even as financial conditions sit at their loosest in a decade, and per the Market list, this was already the worst four-day start to a July for the Nasdaq since 2009. SK Hynix lists Friday, oversubscribed.

1. The capex bill is coming due, and the models are naming names. This is the structural story under the war headline. Three independent cash-flow teardowns landed on the same day, all saying the same thing. Per one Google DCF, Alphabet's operating cash flow soared from $125B to $165B while free cash flow barely moved off $73B, because capex nearly doubled to $91B and guides to $175B or more in 2026, and every single valuation scenario lands below today's price.

Per an Oracle breakdown, $ORCL generated $32B of operating cash and still posted negative $23.7B of free cash flow on data-center spending, and the stock is already down 45% and lower in 19 of the last 24 days. Per a SpaceX short thesis, $SPCX trades near 400 times EBITDA with its balance sheet being poured into hastily built AI data centers. The disease is the same from mega-cap to fresh IPO: the cash goes in and does not come back out.

2. Nvidia's 200-day average is the line that decides the whole tape. Everything hinges on one chart. Per the Market list, $NVDA is sitting right on its 200-day moving average, the level every desk is watching as the hold-or-break for the entire AI trade, and per Stocks with Josh, Michael Burry has increased his short near $198 while the stock grinds out a clean downtrend.

The bull has one strong card left. Per Stocks with Josh, Peter Thiel points out that Nvidia is the only name in this whole complex actually making money, against debt-fueled peers like OpenAI and Anthropic still heading for IPOs, and per the Crypto list Nvidia closed green yesterday even as the sector bled. Hold the 200-day and the rotation was healthy. Lose it and the capex bears get their catalyst.

3. Memory tried to reverse, and the pros cannot agree if it is real. The most crowded trade of the year is the one in question. Per the Stocks list, $MU bounced about 2% overnight as "the Koreans bought the dip," but this is a name that just crashed 25% in two weeks from $1,259 to $938 after being the single best performer in the S&P.

The framing that matters, per the Market list, is Morgan Stanley calling this a peak in the rate of change rather than the end of the cycle, still modeling 35 to 40% earnings growth into 2027 and preferring DRAM over NAND. Per the Financial Markets list, Micron just did $41B of revenue at 85% margins with HBM4 approved for Nvidia's Vera Rubin, so the fundamentals are not the question. The chart is. Watch the reclaim of 966 against support near 810.

4. The pick-and-shovel on the whole buildout is grid power. If the capex is real, the bottleneck is electricity. Per one small-cap thesis, Tecogen $TGEN makes natural-gas-powered chillers now being designed into AI data centers precisely because they do not draw grid power, freeing every saved watt for compute and speeding permitting, and Vertiv is already featuring and installing them. At a $120M market cap with more than a gigawatt showcased, it is a lottery ticket on the exact constraint everyone else is spending into. Per the Polymarket list, AI data-center demand just pushed commercial electricity use past residential for the first time.

5. Bitcoin is basing while everyone waits to buy it 20% lower. The setup keeps quietly improving under the gloom. Per Jesse Eckel, Bitcoin is back to $64,000 after the scare to $57,000, the zone held through the Saylor sale and the Fed fear, and his read is a possible lower low into October before the cycle flips higher. Per the Crypto list, the mood is grim, with Novogratz saying Saylor selling is not a good look and Lyn Alden saying nothing is coming to save Bitcoin, it survives on its own merits. That is what capitulation sounds like, and 17% of all supply has now sat unmoved for ten years.

6. Robotics is the cleanest place the rotation money is looking. The trade leaving crowded chips wants a new story. Per Miles Deutscher, the robotics map runs from the $BOTZ and $ROBO ETFs through Tesla, where Optimus is roughly 80% of the bull case, to the pure-play picks, and the one he details is Ouster $OUST, a lidar maker near $26.80 doing record revenue up 55% as rival Luminar went bankrupt in December. Per the Polymarket list, China alone will build more than 100,000 humanoids this year.

Single names worth your watchlist.

  • $TGEN (Tecogen). Per the thesis, the gas-chiller play on data-center grid constraints, $120M cap, backed by a Vertiv storefront and a growing backlog. Binary and tiny, so size it like the option it is, watching for the first disclosed data-center order.
  • $OUST (Ouster). Per Miles Deutscher, record product revenue up 55%, $175M cash and no debt, plugged into Nvidia's Drive platform, with a DCF near $46 against a high-$20s price. The lidar survivor after Luminar's bankruptcy.
  • $PFE (Pfizer). Per Stocks with Josh, oversold below its pre-COVID price at a 6 to 7% yield, with every prior sub-30 RSI cross rebounding to 60-70 and earnings August 4 that historically front-run by 20 days. The stated buy is $23.
  • $ORCL (Oracle). Per the breakdown, a real battleground: negative free cash flow and a stretched balance sheet against 37% earnings growth at a 0.64 PEG, down 45% and, per Capital Flows, sitting on its 200-week average as the most asymmetric setup in the AI cycle.
  • $SKHY (SK Hynix). The Nasdaq debut lands Friday and is already oversubscribed, the largest ADR listing on record. A cleaner memory vehicle than single-name Micron into a tape that cannot decide if the sector bottomed.

Psychology. Look at what just happened to the crowd's favorite trade. Micron ran 214% to become the single best performer in the S&P this year, and then dropped 25% in two weeks, and this morning, per the Stocks list, the dip-buyers are already stepping back in on one reversal candle. That is the exact reflex that gets punished after a parabola, buying the first green bar because the name made you money on the way up.

Meanwhile Michael Burry is adding to his Nvidia short and three separate analysts just showed the cash flow does not support the prices, and now a war overnight is testing whether the calm was ever real. When the most crowded winner cracks, the 200-day on Nvidia is the line, not your hope that the dip is over.

Now watch Bitcoin do the opposite. Per the Crypto list, Saylor is selling, Novogratz is negative, and Lyn Alden says nothing is coming to save it, and it is holding $64,000 anyway while 17% of supply refuses to move. The two tapes are mirror images. One is a beloved winner everyone is rushing to re-buy on a candle, the other is a hated asset everyone is waiting to buy 20% lower and quietly missing. The discipline today is to distrust your own relief on the semis bounce and to respect the bid under the thing nobody wants. Feelings are loudest exactly where they are most expensive.
