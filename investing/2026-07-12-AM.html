Iran declared the Strait of Hormuz closed overnight, so the calm the market bought all week meets a real oil gap at Monday's open

The IRGC says the strait is shut until further notice after a warning shot, and futures inherit that Monday morning while a fifth of the world's oil hangs on it. Underneath, the memory trade has a new mechanical bull case, Galaxy Digital looks cheap on one data center alone, and the smart money is rotating into Tesla ahead of earnings.

MacroStocksCrypto

Market's closed, so this is a setup, and the setup changed overnight. Iran formally closing the Strait of Hormuz is the kind of headline the tape spent all week deciding not to care about, and now it lands with a fresh number attached. Here is the week ahead, then five stories.

The week ahead. The overnight wildcard is oil. Per Kobeissi on the Stocks list, Iran's Revolutionary Guard declared the Strait of Hormuz closed until further notice late Saturday after firing a warning shot at a vessel, and Monday's open inherits that gap risk with a fifth of the world's seaborne oil moving through that chokepoint. Monday also brings SK Hynix converting to regular-way $SKHY trading and the Senate returning to move the CLARITY Act (the crypto market-structure bill) in its narrow window before the August recess.

Tuesday is the heavier day. June CPI (the inflation print that settles the July 29 Fed debate) hits pre-market, and the $SKHX 2x long and $SKHZ short single-stock ETFs launch the same morning. Earnings season widens with $NFLX, $ASML, and $UNH on the calendar this week, and $TSLA reports July 22. Watch $64,000 on Bitcoin as the binary the Stocks list keeps flagging, a hold points to $70,000 to $71,000 and a loss opens $57,000 to $58,000, with John Gillen leaning toward the lower path in cash.

1. The memory trade has a new bull case, and it is mechanical, not narrative. The read on the Stocks list reframes last week's Micron wobble as a plumbing event, not a thesis break. SK Hynix's Nasdaq listing raised somewhere near $26 to $28 billion and was more than seven times oversubscribed, and funds sold $MU, $SIVE, and $AAOI to free up cash to buy it, on top of thirteen straight days of foreign outflows from Korea worth $15 to $20 billion. If the selling was to fund a subscription rather than to exit the trade, the bounce is mechanical once that flow clears.

The conviction around Micron itself is getting loud. MentoviaX calls this the strongest earnings cycle in the company's history, and Jordi Visser and Milk Road Macro are now floating a $2 trillion Micron within a year with a $4,000 price target. That is the sound of a crowded trade, and the honest way to hold it is to separate the mechanical rebound (real, and tradeable) from the $4,000 call (a story you do not need to believe to own the stock). The torque names riding the same theme, per the same list, are $OPTX, $ASYS, $SIVE, $SHMD, and the interconnect shovels $AAOI, $COHR, $CAMT, $AMKR, and $SNDK.

2. Marvell got a Goldman number that makes the AI-networking trade concrete. Per Melvin on the Stocks list, Goldman now models the AI networking market growing roughly ten-fold from $15 billion to $154 billion by 2028, and $MRVL is the name pointed at it. Marvell did $8.195 billion of revenue in its last fiscal year, up 42 percent, and guides to about $11 billion next year and $15 billion the year after, with the data center now 76 percent of the business and more than 50 custom-chip wins including Google's TPU, Amazon's Trainium, and Meta. This is the same memory-and-compute buildout viewed from the wiring, and the interconnect basket ($AAOI, $SIVE, $SMTC, $CRDO, $MRVL) is where it expresses.

3. The smart money is rotating out of memory and into Tesla ahead of earnings. The rotation call on the Stocks list is explicit: Milk Road Macro moved capital into $TSLA, which ran about 10 percent last week while Micron lagged, and calls it the most popular position on its desk into the July 22 print. The catalyst is real, with Texas legalizing statewide robotaxi operation, so the Cybercab story now has a jurisdiction to prove itself in. This is the same pattern as Friday's neocloud-and-power rotation, money leaving what already ran to buy what has a fresh catalyst, and Tesla into earnings is the highest-beta version of it.

4. Galaxy Digital looks cheap on one data center, and a smaller name sits on a binary. Per the Chairman's Ledger post on the Stocks list, $GLXY (Galaxy Digital) should be worth about $38 on its Helios project alone against roughly $25 today, with a bull case of $41. Helios is an 800-megawatt data center leased to CoreWeave on a fifteen-year contract worth about $900 million of annual EBITDA (operating cash earnings), and a refinancing from 9.1 to about 7 percent would roughly double the free cash flow to equity, with a second tenant expected by end of summer.

The riskier cousin is $NUAI, a behind-the-meter Texas play where a new CEO just bought $5 million of stock at a 20 percent premium. A hyperscaler letter of intent would take it from $5 toward $20 to $30, a five-to-six-times binary that cuts both ways.

5. Amphenol is the boring AI name doing record numbers. Per Rick Orford, $APH (Amphenol) makes the connectors and interconnects that hold an AI rack together, and the business is running hot, record quarterly sales of $7.66 billion up 58 percent, record orders of $9.4 billion, and a book-to-bill of 1.24 (new orders running ahead of shipments, a forward-demand tell). The catch is the price, 42 times earnings and 11 times book, so this is a quality-at-a-premium name rather than a value one, and it is diversified enough across defense, aerospace, and autos that it is not a pure AI bet. Real fundamentals, rich multiple, pick your framework.

Psychology. The whole week was a market teaching itself not to flinch, and this weekend is the test of whether that was discipline or just fatigue. The tape spent five sessions buying into a war, a memory crack, and a short-seller, and by Saturday one trader on the Stocks list had a $2 trillion Micron target on a name that fell 26 percent midweek. Now Iran has actually closed the Strait of Hormuz, and the reflex that worked all week, shrug and buy the dip, is about to meet a real oil gap at Monday's open.

That is the trap of a crowd that got rewarded for ignoring risk. The lesson it learned was that nothing matters, and the one time that lesson is most expensive is the first time something does. If you are tempted to pre-load Monday longs into the memory names or Tesla because the dip has paid every time, remember you would be buying the most crowded positioning in years into an oil shock nobody has priced.

So the discipline check this weekend is simple. The mechanical Micron rebound and the Tesla rotation are real trades with real catalysts, but they are not reasons to be maximally long into a Monday that could open with crude gapping and the whole complacency trade getting its first honest test. Size for the gap, keep the levels in front of you, $64,000 on Bitcoin, $20 on SoFi, $7.30 on POET, and let the open tell you whether the crowd was right to stop caring.
