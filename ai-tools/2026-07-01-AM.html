Commerce lifts the leash on Claude, OpenAI freezes its IPO, and the AI bubble shows up in the earnings

The Department of Commerce restored export access to Fable 5 and Mythos 5, OpenAI pushed its IPO toward 2027 while Anthropic races to list first, and a new study says a chunk of S&P profit is just AI paper gains

ModelsComputeTooling

Three stories run the AI tape this morning and they are all about access and money, not benchmarks. Commerce took the leash off Anthropic's frontier models. OpenAI slammed the brakes on its IPO. And a finance professor put a number on how much of the market's AI profit is imaginary. Benchmarks can wait.

Commerce restored export access to Fable 5 and Mythos 5

The biggest catalyst of the session: the Department of Commerce lifted the export controls on Anthropic's Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5, and access is restoring (Market list). Politico had flagged the easing was coming. This showed up across five separate feeds this morning, so it is real and it matters. The models that were throttled are coming back online, which unwinds one of the biggest overhangs on Anthropic right as the company is trying to walk into public markets. If you use the Claude frontier tier, expect capacity to loosen.

OpenAI hit pause on its IPO, and Anthropic is racing to go first

OpenAI filed a confidential S-1 on June 8 and was on track for a fall debut. Now it is pushing toward 2027 (Prof G Markets). The reasons are unflattering. Sam Altman wants a $1 trillion valuation, CFO Sarah Friar said the company is not ready, and Alex Heath's reporting describes the finance and investor-relations org as not in place, disclosures as "a mess," Sam owning no equity, and governance "all over the place." SpaceX's rocky IPO day spooked them into waiting.

That opens the door for Anthropic to list first, with people eyeing October or November and Kalshi putting the odds of Anthropic going public this year at 76 percent (Prof G Markets). The logic is simple and a little brutal. The third AI IPO gets less demand than the second, so whoever goes first wins the scarcity premium. Scott Galloway reframes the real question as whether these deals even "get away," since both firms can raise enough privately to never need the public market (Prof G Markets). If they keep the ship in harbor, that tells you the window closed.

The AI bubble is now visible in the accounting

Here is the number to save. University of Florida finance professor Baolin Wang found that Q1 2026 S&P earnings were inflated by roughly 12 percent (Prof G Markets). Alphabet, Amazon, and Nvidia booked a combined $69 billion in profit from unrealized gains on their OpenAI and Anthropic stakes, run through "other income" under accounting standard ASU 2016-01, not from selling anything. Strip out the markups and S&P earnings growth drops to about 16 percent, right in line with the five-year average. Private AI valuations now account for roughly 12 cents of every profit dollar in the S&P. Wang's line: "our main bubble indicator is now also in a bubble." This is the cleanest bear case in the market and it belongs on your radar.

Models: the frontier keeps moving

  • GPT-5.6 is projected to ship within 10 days at 61 percent odds on Polymarket (Polymarket list). Anthropic already shipped Claude Sonnet 5 and a Claude Science app.
  • A real capability milestone: GPT-5.5 Pro paired with Claude Opus 4.8 resolved 9 open math problems, including 4 from COLT, 1 from FOCS, and 4 in commutative algebra (Hermes list). Frontier models solving genuinely open problems is the story under the IPO noise.

Tooling: Midjourney walks into healthcare

Midjourney, a 40-person bootstrapped shop doing $200 million in revenue and highly profitable, is plowing cash into preventative medicine (AI News and Strategy Daily). The product is a cheap ultrasound device that images the body far faster than an MRI, which they are calling the biggest medical-imaging breakthrough in 50 years, with a goal of roughly 1 billion scans a year within a couple of years. Big claim from an image-generation company. Worth watching precisely because they have the profitability to actually fund it.

Compute: the picks-and-shovels bid, plus a Leopold sighting

The money is chasing whoever can serve cheap inference and power it. Leopold Aschenbrenner's Situational Awareness took a 19.9 percent stake in Sharon AI ($SHAZ), an Australian neocloud with a 72MW Nvidia deal and roughly $1.3 billion in ARR, deliberately staying just under the 20 percent equity-method accounting line (Crypto list). Bloom Energy's AI-power deal got expanded from $5 billion to $25 billion (Financial Markets list). Nvidia put $1 billion into Nokia for AI networking. And the counterweight to all the hiring hype: Microsoft is laying off more than 5,000 (Polymarket list). The AI capex boom and the AI headcount cut are happening in the same company at the same time. That is the whole labor story in one line.
