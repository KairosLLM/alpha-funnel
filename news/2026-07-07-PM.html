Iran's missiles in the Strait of Hormuz hit a Qatari gas tanker, and Trump remade a chunk of foreign policy in a single afternoon

The Gulf strike put energy shipping back in the crosshairs, the President lifted Turkey's sanctions and floated taking Greenland, and the Democratic Party's civil war got mapped out in real time. Here is what moved after the AM edition.

PoliticsWorldMarkets

The morning left Iran as a headline risk. By evening it was a hit ship, a scrambled map of American foreign policy, and a Democratic Party openly at war with itself. Here is the update.

1. Iran's Hormuz strike hit a Qatari gas tanker, and that changes the stakes. The escalation got specific. Per the Polymarket list and the Crypto list, Iran fired at least two missiles at ships in the Strait of Hormuz overnight and struck a Qatari LNG carrier, pulling liquefied natural gas into the same risk premium that already sits on oil. A missile that hits empty water is a warning. One that hits a Gulf ally's gas tanker is an event, and it puts the energy-price tail risk back on the table just as inflation expectations were already climbing.

2. Trump remade foreign policy in an afternoon. Three moves, one day. Per the Polymarket list, Trump lifted all US sanctions on Turkey and opened the door to selling it F-35s ahead of a bilateral with Erdogan in Ankara, and separately said Greenland "should be controlled by the United States." Taken together it is a sharp pivot toward transactional, territory-minded dealmaking, rewarding a NATO partner that had been frozen out while reviving a claim most people had written off as a first-term provocation.

3. The Democratic Party's civil war is now out in the open. Two threads pointed at the same fracture. Per the All-In Podcast, Nate Silver mapped the party into three warring camps, the progressive left of Zohran Mamdani, AOC, and Bernie Sanders, the free-market "abundance" liberals around Ezra Klein, and the "resistance" liberals who believe Biden was railroaded out and who form Gavin Newsom's base.

The Maine Senate race is where the fight turned bloody. Per the Polymarket list, Democrat Graham Platner is now better than 70% to drop out by tomorrow after Mamdani publicly called for him to quit and a new market opened on whether he gets criminally charged, though per the Kalshi list the drop-out odds actually cooled from 83% to 60% intraday. Either way the Democrats still hold the seat at 57%.

4. Around the world, quickly.

  • Syria. Per the Crypto list, a twin bombing in Damascus near the Four Seasons and Macron's hotel wounded 18, a jarring strike during a high-profile diplomatic visit.
  • France. Per the same list, Marine Le Pen has been cleared to run in 2027, back in the race while wearing an ankle monitor.
  • United Kingdom. Per the Crypto list, Nigel Farage resigned his seat as an MP, triggering a Clacton by-election.
  • United States, corporate. Microsoft cut its entire Xbox division, roughly 5,500 to 6,400 jobs, another reminder that the layoffs are landing in the parts of Big Tech that are not the AI story.
  • United States, markets. Per Prof G Markets, Dell added about a billion dollars of market value within hours after Trump told people on live television to "go buy a Dell," a stock his own disclosures show he bought nearly a million dollars of last year.
