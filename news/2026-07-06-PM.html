Washington started depositing 1,000 dollars into every newborn's brokerage account, and billionaires lined up to add to the pot

Trump Accounts went live over the holiday with rare bipartisan backing and billions in private pledges, Russia hit Kyiv the day after a call with Trump, and the AI layoffs arrived with a very careful denial. Here is what moved after the AM edition.

PoliticsWorldMarkets

The morning was quiet, the evening filled in the edges. A new government program put investment accounts in the hands of every American child, the war in Ukraine escalated right after a leaders' call, and a fresh round of tech layoffs came wrapped in a careful message about who is really being replaced. Here is the update.

1. The government is now seeding a brokerage account for every newborn, and the money is bipartisan. This is the domestic story of the weekend. Per Anthony Pompliano, Trump Accounts went live on July 4, a tax-deferred custodial account that drops a one-time $1,000 government deposit into an S&P 500 fund for every US child born between 2025 and 2028, with families able to add up to $5,000 a year and the balance locked until age 18 for college, a business, or a home.

The private money piled in on top. Michael and Susan Dell pledged $6.25B to seed accounts for 25 million children, Gwynne Shotwell and her husband committed 2 million SpaceX shares worth more than $300M, and JPMorgan, Bank of America, and Coinbase each rolled out programs to match another $1,000 per employee child. Per the People-to-follow list, Trump personally rang in the launch for 1.4 million accounts. The rare part is the politics: this drew support across the aisle, a genuine attempt to hand the next generation a stake in the market rather than an argument about it.

2. Russia hit Kyiv the day after a Putin-Trump call, and Ukraine struck deep into Russia in return. The war took a harder turn. Per the Polymarket list, Russia killed at least eight people in a barrage on Kyiv just after a July 4 phone call between Putin and Trump, the kind of timing that reads as a message rather than a coincidence. Ukraine answered with its deepest strike of the war, hitting an oil refinery in Omsk some 2,500 kilometers inside Russia, extending its campaign against the fuel that moves the Russian military. A call meant to lower the temperature was followed within a day by an escalation on both ends.

3. The AI layoffs arrived with a careful denial, and the squeeze underneath it is real. The job cuts came, and so did the messaging around them. Per the People-to-follow list, Microsoft is cutting about 4,800 jobs, roughly 2.1% of its workforce, and its HR chief Amy Coleman went out of her way to say those workers are not being replaced by AI, while Porsche announced 4,000 cuts in Germany. The denial is doing a lot of work in a week when every headline is about automation.

Underneath the layoffs is a slower grind. Per ClearValue Tax, wages are up 23% since 2021 while prices are up 28%, property insurance has climbed 75% in five years, housing now eats more than 40% of the typical income, and credit card debt has hit a record $1.25T with delinquencies at their highest since 2008. The Trump Accounts are a bet on the next generation precisely because this generation cannot get ahead.

4. Around the world, quickly.

  • China. Per the Polymarket list, Beijing test-fired a ballistic missile from a nuclear submarine into the Pacific, a rare and deliberate show of reach.
  • China, again. Per the same list, regulators forced ByteDance and Alibaba to pull AI-companion features from their apps, a sharp turn against the consumer AI its own firms are building.
  • Saudi Arabia. Per the same list, Riyadh cut its August Arab Light price to Asia by $11 a barrel, the largest such cut in more than 26 years, a signal it is fighting for market share as OPEC+ lifts output.
  • United States. Per a Prof G Markets clip, Scott Galloway used the fight over rolling back 14th Amendment birthright citizenship to needle Stephen Miller and Trump, noting that under the proposed change he, the son of immigrants, would not be a citizen, and joking he would like his decade of taxes back.
