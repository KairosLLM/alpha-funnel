Iran declared the ceasefire framework dead as the strikes rolled on, gas is heading back toward $4, and Trump's media company is selling fast access to the president's market-moving posts

Tehran said it will no longer honor the 14-point understanding and widened its target list to Saudi and Kuwaiti sites, while US airstrikes continued and diesel crossed $5 a gallon. Trump Media pitched a $100,000-a-month feed to front-run the president's posts, and a devastating earthquake hit Venezuela.

A war that just tore up its own truce, a governance story hiding in plain sight, and a natural disaster. Ranked.

1. Iran declared the ceasefire understanding dead and widened the war. The diplomatic track collapsed. Per the Financial Markets list, Iran accused the US of breaking a 14-point memorandum by seizing parts of the Strait of Hormuz and striking its infrastructure, then formally declared the understanding dead and said it would no longer fulfill its obligations, as US airstrikes hit Hormozgan province and the Gulf coast town of Sirik.

The target list is expanding beyond Iran itself. Per the Market list, Iran added a Saudi base and a Kuwaiti water and desalination plant to its list, Kuwaiti crews spent the day containing fires from earlier strikes, and shrapnel landed in residential areas. The cost is now showing up at the pump, with the average price of US diesel crossing $5 a gallon and regular gasoline about to breach $4, per Mohamed El-Erian's read of the AAA data. A war fought over the world's most important oil chokepoint is finally reaching the American driveway.

2. Trump's media company is selling a head start on the president's posts. The quietest story may be the most striking. Per the Stocks list, Trump Media is pitching a service that would charge $100,000 a month for a fast feed of the president's social posts, which repeatedly move stocks the moment they land.

The framing writes itself, that this is a fee to front-run the front-running of a sitting president. The Wall Street Journal has documented case after case of a single Truth Social post swinging individual stocks, so a paid low-latency feed turns the president's words into a tradable data product sold to whoever can afford the subscription. It is legal as described and it is also a clean picture of how thoroughly market access and political power have merged.

3. A major earthquake devastated Venezuela. The humanitarian headline of the weekend is grim. Per the Financial Markets list, a powerful earthquake in Venezuela has left more than 5,000 people dead and as many as 50,000 missing, a catastrophe unfolding in a country already stretched thin. The scale of the missing suggests the toll will climb before it settles.

4. Around the world, quickly.

  • GameStop is now a major eBay shareholder. The one-time meme stock now owns nearly 10 percent of eBay, per the Polymarket list. The retail-trading saga keeps writing strange new chapters.
  • The World Cup final is set, and the bets are enormous. Argentina faces Spain in the final, and the musician Drake put $1.5 million on Argentina to win, a wager that pays more than $5 million if it lands, per the Polymarket list. The tournament ends with a bet bigger than most prize purses.
  • A long-hunted fugitive was caught. A man on the America's Most Wanted list for more than 20 years was captured off the coast of New Jersey, per the Polymarket list. A two-decade search closed over a summer weekend.
