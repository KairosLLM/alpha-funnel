The midterm map moved toward Republicans, and the money noticed first

Prediction markets now give the GOP a clear edge to hold the Senate, California cut its own AI deal ahead of Washington, and Russia is massing for a big strike on Ukraine.

PoliticsWorldMarkets

1. The Senate picture swung, and the shift is the story. On Kalshi, Republicans are now 59% to hold the Senate, up 8 points since May 1, with Democrats down to 41% from 49%. That is the cleanest tradeable political move on the board and it happened without a single big headline, which is usually how the real ones move. Underneath the topline the races are more mixed than the number suggests: Jon Ossoff sits at 86% to hold Georgia, Maine has Platner tied with Collins, Ken Paxton leads in Texas at 58%, and in the Georgia governor's race Jackson leads at 54%. The wildest intraday move was the Colorado GOP gubernatorial primary, where Victor Marx ran from 57% to 90% in a session. Looking past the midterms, the 2028 Democratic nominee market is a genuine scrum: Newsom 22%, Ossoff at a new high of 14%, AOC 14%, Harris down to 8%. File the Senate shift as the one that matters now.

2. California didn't wait for Washington on AI. The return of Anthropic's flagship model from an 18-day export ban came with a governance tell worth flagging, per AI Central: California signed its own deal with Anthropic rather than wait for federal approval, and the redeployment arrived alongside a "consensus framework" that Anthropic is drafting with Amazon, Microsoft, and Google to score jailbreak severity, plus expanded US-government model testing. The through-line is that access to frontier AI is now a thing governments negotiate and gate, state by state and lab by lab, not a pure market. Palantir's Alex Karp spent the same news cycle needling the labs in public, arguing enterprises are paying for tokens that deliver no lasting value. The product mechanics of all this live in AI & Tools. The point for News is simpler: AI policy is now being written by states and standards bodies in real time.

3. Trump put quantum on the national-priority list. A new executive order pushes quantum computing to the center of US strategy, framed less as a funding announcement and more as a declaration that quantum is now a competitiveness and national-security priority on par with semiconductors, per Lark Davis. The pitch invokes problems that would take classical or AI systems ages, solved in minutes, across drug discovery, cryptography, and military logistics. Take the timelines with salt and note the order names no companies. The signal is the policy posture, not any specific stock, which is exactly why it sits here and not in Investing.

4. The Fed is changing character, quietly. The most underpriced institutional shift is at the Fed itself. The read on the Warsh regime is that it kills the print-to-print obsession, with Warsh reportedly pledging 2% inflation eight times in a single press conference and yields falling in response. That matters for how you read every data release for the next two years, trend over noise. The market is not fully buying the dovish story, Kalshi still prices a 50% chance of a rate hike this year. The tradeable market implications are in Investing.

5. Around the world, quickly. From Polymarket and the wires: Russia is readying a large drone and missile strike on Ukraine, reportedly up to 800 drones plus Iskander-M and Zircon missiles. Taiwan tensions are simmering, with Japan's Takaichi heading to India and wealthy Taiwanese quietly moving assets to Singapore. Crude flow through the Strait of Hormuz surged to roughly 10M barrels a day even as oil prices stayed soft. Markets abroad wobbled, with Korea's KOSPI down about 6% and the Korea Exchange halting program selling. On trade, the US declined to extend USMCA. And a 2025 disclosure showed Trump making up to $1.4B in stock purchases across roughly 22,000 trades, with more than $635M tied to World Liberty Financial and a meme coin, the kind of number that will be its own story soon enough.
