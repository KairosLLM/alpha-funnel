Iran says the Strait of Hormuz is closed until further notice, turning a week of threats into an actual act

The Revolutionary Guard declared the strait shut late Saturday after firing a warning shot at a passing vessel, the hard version of the deadline that came and went with no declaration. It is a quiet overnight otherwise, with the prediction markets already looking three years ahead to a 2028 race.

WorldPoliticsMarkets

A light overnight with one heavy item. All week Iran and Washington traded deadlines and threats over the Strait of Hormuz without either side acting on them. Late Saturday that changed.

1. Iran formally closed the Strait of Hormuz, the escalation the week kept not delivering. Per Kobeissi on the Stocks list, Iran's Revolutionary Guard announced it is closing the Strait of Hormuz until further notice after firing a warning shot at a passing vessel late Saturday night. This is the concrete version of everything that stayed rhetorical all week, the reopening deadline that passed Saturday with a thousand-missile threat instead of a declaration now answered with a closure instead of a handshake.

The strait carries roughly a fifth of the world's seaborne oil, so a real closure is a market event as much as a military one, and futures inherit it at Monday's open. The caveat worth keeping is that Iran has threatened to close Hormuz many times over the years and the physical reality of enforcing it against the US Fifth Fleet is another matter, so the question into Monday is whether this is an announcement or an actual blockade. Either way, the market that spent the week deciding the war did not matter now has to decide again with the chokepoint officially shut.

2. With little else moving, the prediction markets jumped to 2028. A widely shared AI-generated 2028 forecast is mostly filler, a model roleplaying invented margins, but it surfaced the real prediction-market odds underneath. On Kalshi, JD Vance sits at 41 percent to be the Republican nominee and Gavin Newsom at 19 percent to be the Democratic favorite, with Newsom running behind a surging field on his own side. Three years out those numbers are noise as forecasts and signal as a snapshot of where the smart money's attention already is, on Vance as the clear Republican front-runner and a genuinely open Democratic race. Treat it as a temperature reading, not a result.
